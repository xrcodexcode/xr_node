# Reality of 20th July Protest
Source: [YT](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0j0vUAo52PM&rco=1)
![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0j0vUAo52PM)

Support Us: https://show.sarthakgoswami.com/web/checkout/69b27275a9095b101d825623  
  
On July 20, 2026 — a day many now call Black Monday — thousands of young Indians marched toward Parliament on the opening day of the Monsoon Session, under the banner of the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP), a movement born from an insult, demanding answers on the NEET-UG 2026 exam paper leak, unemployment, and the future of an entire generation. By evening, Delhi's streets told a different story: tear gas, lathi charges, more than 150 reported injured, dozens detained.  
  
We were there. On the ground, cameras rolling — from the first slogans at Jantar Mantar to the chaos near Parliament.  
  
In this documentary, we break down:  
▸ The questions this generation is asking the government — and democracy itself  
▸ The full timeline of July 20 — how a march that began with songs and slogans descended into tear gas and chaos  
▸ Sonam Wangchuk — his 21-day hunger strike, his hospitalisation, and his handwritten plea to join the march  
▸ The emotional arc of the day — from hope and celebration to fear and heartbreak  
▸ What mainstream media showed — and what students on the ground lived through  
▸ How independent journalists and creators documented the reality  
▸ Where the movement goes from here  
  
This is not just a protest story. It's a record of a generation demanding to be heard.
