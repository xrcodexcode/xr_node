# Why You Are Feeling STUCK In Your 20s | Warikoo Careers Hindi
Source: [YT](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=06aGxheLHAQ&list=WL&index=2)

You have a degree, a job, and access to more information and advice than any generation in history - ChatGPT, Google, endless content, people to ask. On paper, you should feel more equipped than ever. So why do you wake up most mornings with a feeling of anxiety, convinced you're falling behind while everyone else has quietly figured life out? Having spoken to, mentored, interviewed and invested in thousands of people over the years, I've come to believe this generation deserves one title above all - the most confused generation ever. In this video, I want to show you why that confusion isn't your fault, why it's a reality you'll have to face, and how to finally get out of it.  

The root of it is surprising: you're not confused because you have too few options - you're confused because you have too many. Unlike someone with no safety net who has absolute clarity because survival demands it, you have choices, a cushion, and social media constantly amplifying every possible path. So I make an argument most people won't - keeping your options open is actually bad advice, because there's a huge difference between confusion and diversification, and optionality is a kind of poison that stops you from ever committing 100% to anything. I break down Greg McKeown's "hell yes or no" filter and Jeff Bezos's distinction between reversible and irreversible decisions. I close with my own story - coming back from the US at 24 on a full scholarship with no plan and the single principle I've run my life on for 21 years - keep moving. Watch till the end, because the person telling you you're stuck is your biggest enemy - and that person might be you.
