# Master the Art of Timing

## Core idea
Never seem to be in a hurry—hurrying betrays a lack of self-control, and of control over time. Always seem patient, as if you know that everything will come to you eventually. Become a detective of the right moment; sniff out the spirit of the times, the trends that will carry you to power. Learn to stand back when the time is not yet ripe, and to strike fiercely when it has reached fruition.

## Why it matters / connection
Joseph Fouché survived the French Revolution, the Reign of Terror, Directory, Consulate, Empire, and restoration of the monarchy by mastering the art of timing. He remained quiet when factions were strong, waiting for them to exhaust themselves, and switched sides only when the momentum was clear, ensuring he remained minister of police under every regime.
