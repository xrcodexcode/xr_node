# Environment Design

## Core idea
**Environment Design** is the practice of structuring your physical and digital surroundings to make the cues for desired habits highly obvious and visible, while making the cues for undesirable habits hard to find or invisible.

### The Science of Perception
- **Lewin's Equation**: Psychologist Kurt Lewin wrote that behavior is a function of the person and their environment: $B = f(P, E)$.
- **Visual Dominance**: Humans possess approximately eleven million sensory receptors, and about ten million of those are dedicated to sight. Because half of the brain's resources are used on vision, visual cues are the greatest catalyst of human behavior.
- **Suggestion Impulse Buying**: Consumers buy products not because they need them, but because of how they are presented (e.g., items at eye-level or end caps on aisles drive 45% of Coca-Cola sales).

### Key Strategy: One Space, One Use
When contexts are mixed (e.g., working from the couch, watching TV in bed), habits get mixed, and the easier ones (watching TV, browsing social media) always win. To counter this, assign specific activities to specific areas:
- **Redefine Zones**: Dedicate a chair only for reading, a desk only for writing, and a table only for eating.
- **Digital Spaces**: Use a computer only for work, a tablet only for reading, and a phone only for messaging.
- *Stable environments make for stable habits.*

### Actionable Techniques & Examples
- **Remembering Medication**: Place the pill bottle directly next to the faucet on the bathroom counter.
- **Practicing an Instrument**: Place the guitar on a stand in the middle of the living room instead of tucked in a closet.
- **Healthy Eating**: Keep a display bowl of fresh apples on the kitchen counter instead of hidden in the refrigerator crisper drawer.
- **Drinking Water**: Fill up several water bottles in the morning and scatter them in common rooms around the house.
- **The Dutch Energy Study (1970s)**: Homeowners with electrical meters located in the main hallway used 30% less energy than those with meters in the basement because their consumption was visible and obvious.
- **Schiphol Airport Urinal Flies**: Putting fly stickers in the center of urinals in Amsterdam's airport improved aim, reducing spillage and cutting bathroom cleaning costs by 8% per year.

## Why it matters / connection
Most people live in environments designed by others. By practicing environment design, you transition from being a consumer of your world to being the architect of it. It removes reliance on willpower by embedding triggers directly into your physical environment, ensuring the desired habit is the path of least resistance.
