# Scarcity Bias in Investing

## Core idea
Scarcity Bias is the psychological tendency to place a higher value on assets that are perceived to be rare, limited, or urgent, often leading to emotional, rushed purchase decisions.

## Why it matters / connection
Wall Street underwriters exploit scarcity bias during IPOs or hot mergers by creating artificial urgency ('limited allocation'). Value investors counter this by ignoring market-induced FOMO, waiting patiently for stable, highly liquid entry points where they have structural margins of safety.
