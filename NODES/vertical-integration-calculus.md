# Vertical Integration Calculus

Build in-house when the idiot index is high or suppliers cannot meet your requirements. Outsource only when the market is already efficient.

## Explanation

Musk's vertical integration strategy is a direct output of first principles thinking and the Idiot Index. Traditional automakers outsource ~70% of parts. Tesla builds vastly more in-house: seats, electronics, software, batteries, even its own semiconductor chips.

The calculus:
- **High Idiot Index** (supplier price >> material cost) → build in-house
- **Strategic dependency** (supplier bottleneck risk) → build in-house
- **Innovation requirement** (suppliers can't meet specs) → build in-house
- **Low Idiot Index / commodity market** → outsource

SpaceX applies the same: they manufacture ~85% of rocket components in-house, including engines, avionics, and even the metal alloys.

## Why it matters

Vertical integration gives Musk control over cost, quality, and iteration speed. It allows rapid prototyping without supplier negotiations. The trade-off is higher capital intensity and operational complexity.
