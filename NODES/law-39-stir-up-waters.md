# Stir Up Waters to Catch Fish

## Core idea
Anger and emotion are strategically counterproductive. You must always stay calm and objective. If you can make your enemies angry while staying calm yourself, you gain a decisive advantage. Rattle your enemies: Find the chink in their vanity through which you can anger them and make them lose their grip.

## Why it matters / connection
Napoleon was deliberately goaded into anger by Talleyrand, who would remain silent and impassive during the Emperor's loud outbursts. By making Napoleon lose his cool, Talleyrand exposed the Emperor's strategic panic, making him look weak in front of his ministers and foreign diplomats, while Talleyrand appeared in complete control.
