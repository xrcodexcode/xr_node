# Bias from Association

## Core idea
Bias from Association is the psychological tendency to evaluate a product, brand, or person based on the qualities of associated items or experiences, rather than its independent merits.

## Why it matters / connection
Companies spend billions on advertising to create positive associations (e.g., linking a soda to happiness and sports). Buffett uses this bias to evaluate brand equity: if a consumer strongly associates a brand with trust and happiness, the company can charge premium prices, establishing a powerful moat.
