# Machiavelli and The Prince

## Core idea
Niccolò Machiavelli's *The Prince* (1513) is the foundational text of realist political philosophy. It analyzes power as it is actually exercised, rather than how it should be in an ideal world, emphasizing virtù (boldness/skill) and the necessity of strategic ruthlessness.

## Why it matters / connection
Machiavelli argued that a ruler must know how to enter into evil if forced by necessity, and that it is far safer to be feared than loved, as love is fickle while fear is maintained by the dread of punishment. He held up Cesare Borgia as a prime model of political virtù. Robert Greene's *The 48 Laws of Power* is a modern adaptation of this realist framework, stripping away moralistic judgments to analyze the dynamics of control.
