# Never Appear Too Perfect

## Core idea
Appearing better than others is always dangerous, but most dangerous of all is to appear to have no faults or weaknesses. Envy creates silent enemies. It is smart to occasionally display defects, and admit to harmless vices, in order to deflect envy and appear more human and approachable. Only gods and the dead can seem perfect with impunity.

## Why it matters / connection
Cosimo de' Medici avoided the fate of other wealthy Florentines by dressing modestly, riding a mule instead of a fine horse, and refusing public offices. He kept his immense power hidden behind a mask of simplicity, deflecting the envy that destroyed his rivals. Conversely, playwright Joe Orton flaunted his success and superiority, provoking intense envy in his partner who eventually killed him.
