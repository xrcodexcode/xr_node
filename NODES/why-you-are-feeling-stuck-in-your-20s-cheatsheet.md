# Cheatsheet — Why You Are Feeling STUCK In Your 20s

This cheatsheet provides a high-density reference of the core models and arguments from Ankur Warikoo's career guidance transcript.

## Core Summary

- **The Problem:** The current generation of young adults (20–28) faces paralysis and anxiety not due to too few options, but due to an abundance of choices combined with safety nets and social media noise.
- **The Solution:** Restrict optionality, enforce a high-threshold filter for commitments, categorise choices by reversibility, and maintain constant motion.

---

## Key Frameworks & Statistics

### 1. The Paradox of Choice
- **Statistic:** Indian unemployment rate for graduates is ~13%, while illiterate unemployment is near 0%.
- **Insight:** Lacking a safety net creates absolute clarity of purpose (survival). Possessing a safety net (parental sponsorship, cushion) enables deferred commitment and leads to paralysis.

### 2. Optionality is Poison
- **Diversification vs. Confusion:** Diversification requires one stable, anchored base option. Trying to do multiple uncommitted paths simultaneously creates confusion.
- **Commitment Trap:** The mere illusion of open options prevents 100% commitment to any single path.

### 3. Greg McKeown's Filter
- **Rule:** If it is not a **"Hell Yes!"**, the default answer must be **"No."**
- **Purpose:** Avoid letting mediocre options consume your focus and energy.

### 4. Bezos's Decision Doors
- **One-Way Doors (Irreversible):** Deliberate slowly and carefully (e.g., quitting a job, breaking relationships).
- **Two-Way Doors (Reversible):** Decide instantly; pivot if it doesn't work (e.g., subscribing to courses, moving cities). 99% of decisions are two-way doors.

### 5. Keep Moving
- **Heuristic:** Directional course corrections are only possible when you are in motion. Pick a path and start moving.
- **Definition:** True "stuckness" is a luxury of choice. Act to break the loop.
