# Commitment and Consistency Bias

## Core idea
Commitment and Consistency Bias is the tendency to align one's future actions and beliefs with past public commitments and decisions to maintain a coherent self-image and avoid social discomfort.

## Why it matters / connection
In investing, this bias makes it extremely difficult for managers to sell a failing stock because doing so requires admitting they made a mistake in their original public thesis. Buffett manages this by conducting objective, rational reviews and accepting that some investments (like Dexter Shoe) will fail.
