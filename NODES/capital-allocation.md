# Rational Capital Allocation

## Core idea
Capital Allocation is the process of deciding how to deploy a firm's financial resources (earnings and capital) to maximize long-term shareholder value. It is the primary responsibility of a corporate executive.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett believes that most CEOs are excellent at operations or sales but have poor capital allocation skills. A CEO has five options for deploying cash:
1. Reinvest in existing operations (organic growth).
2. Acquire other businesses.
3. Pay down debt.
4. Pay dividends.
5. Buy back company stock.
Buffett's genius was treating Berkshire Hathaway as a capital reallocation vehicle. He took cash flows from low-growth or mature businesses (like textiles and insurance) and funneled them into high-return acquisitions or stock portfolios. This structural reallocation maximizes compounding interest by ensuring earnings are never left idle or wasted on low-return operations.
