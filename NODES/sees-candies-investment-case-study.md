# See's Candies Case Study

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway's acquisition of See's Candies in 1972 for $25 million was the prototype investment that validated buying high-quality businesses with pricing power and brand equity (an economic moat) rather than cheap asset-heavy businesses.

## Why it matters / connection
Before See's, Buffett focused on buying cheap "cigar butt" companies. See's Candies was a premium chocolate brand with loyal customers in California. Its physical assets were worth only $8 million, meaning Berkshire paid a significant premium for "goodwill." However, See's had extraordinary brand loyalty, allowing it to raise prices every year without losing volume. This pricing power required very little capital reinvestment to grow, generating massive cash flows. Berkshire used these cash flows to fund other high-return acquisitions, demonstrating how brand-based moats act as cash engines for compounding.
