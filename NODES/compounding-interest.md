# Long-Term Compounding

## Core idea
Compounding is the process where the value of an asset grows exponentially over time because the returns generated by the asset are reinvested to generate their own returns, forming a compounding loop.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett describes compounding as the primary secret of his wealth, noting that his life is an illustration of "the snowball effect." Compounding requires two ingredients: high returns and a long time horizon. Because Buffett started investing at age 11 and has continued for over 80 years, over 99% of his wealth was accumulated after his 50th birthday. 
In Berkshire's operations, this is achieved by retaining 100% of earnings (Berkshire pays no dividend) and reinvesting them, allowing capital to compound internally without being diminished by double taxation.
