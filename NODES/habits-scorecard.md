# Habits Scorecard

## Core idea
The Habits Scorecard is a simple self-awareness exercise used to identify and track daily behaviors. To create one, write down a sequential list of all daily habits (e.g., wake up, turn off alarm, check phone, take a shower, brush teeth). Once the list is compiled, categorize each behavior as a good habit (`+`), a bad habit (`-`), or a neutral habit (`=`).

The classification of a habit depends entirely on the individual's current goals and desired identity. For instance, eating a bagel with peanut butter might be marked as a bad habit (`-`) for someone trying to lose weight, but a good habit (`+`) for someone trying to build muscle. To resolve ambiguous habits, ask: "Does this behavior help me become the type of person I wish to be? Does this habit cast a vote for or against my desired identity?"

When creating the scorecard, the goal is not to immediately change any behaviors, but simply to observe thoughts and actions without judgment or self-criticism.

## Why it matters / connection
The process of behavior change always starts with awareness. Because firmly rooted habits become nonconscious and automatic, the Habits Scorecard serves as a tool to map current routines. It makes the invisible cues and automated actions visible so they can be effectively modified.
