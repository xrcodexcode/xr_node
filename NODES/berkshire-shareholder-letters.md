# Berkshire Shareholder Letters

## Core idea
Every year since 1965, Warren Buffett has written a detailed, candid letter to Berkshire Hathaway shareholders. These letters serve as a medium for educating shareholders on investment theory, corporate governance, and business ethics, while enforcing management transparency.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett uses a unique, conversational writing style (aiming his letters at his sisters, who represent intelligent lay-investors) to demystify complex accounting and investment rules. By openly discussing both successes and errors (such as the original Berkshire textile purchase or buying Dexter Shoe), Buffett builds deep trust with his capital base. This transparency acts as a competitive advantage, attracting long-term shareholders who do not panic during market cycles, giving Berkshire a stable capital structure for compounding.
