# Berkshire's Insurance Float

## Core idea
Insurance Float is the cash collected from policyholders in premiums before any claims are paid out. Berkshire Hathaway utilizes this float as a source of cost-free or low-cost investment capital to purchase high-yielding businesses and stocks.

## Why it matters / connection
In traditional insurance companies, float is invested in highly conservative bonds. Buffett realized that because Berkshire's insurance subsidiaries (like GEICO and National Indemnity) are managed with strict underwriting discipline—often generating an underwriting profit—the float is essentially "free money" that is highly unlikely to shrink. Instead of keeping all float in bonds, Buffett reallocates it to purchase high-quality equity investments and entire businesses. This leverage model compounds Berkshire's capital at an accelerated rate, acting as the primary engine for Berkshire's growth.
