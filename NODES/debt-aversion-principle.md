# Debt Aversion Principle

## Core idea
The Debt Aversion Principle states that businesses and individuals should minimize or avoid debt because leverage introduces the risk of bankruptcy during economic downturns, whereas unleveraged businesses are mathematically immune to insolvency.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett has consistently run Berkshire Hathaway with significant cash reserves and minimal debt, noting that 'it is crazy to risk what you have and need for what you don't have and don't need.' This conservative approach ensures survival during financial crises, allowing Berkshire to act as a liquidity provider when competitors are failing.
