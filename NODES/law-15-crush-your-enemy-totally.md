# Crush Your Enemy Totally

## Core idea
All great leaders since Moses have known that a feared enemy must be crushed completely. If one ember is left alight, no matter how dimly it smolders, a fire will eventually break out. More is lost through stopgap measures and sympathy than through total annihilation: The enemy will recover, and will seek revenge. Crush him, not only in body but in spirit.

## Why it matters / connection
In ancient China, the general Hsiang Yu spared his rival Liu Pang at a banquet, feeling sympathy. Liu Pang escaped, gathered his forces, and ultimately defeated and destroyed Hsiang Yu, establishing the Han Dynasty. Robert Greene notes that leaving an enemy alive means they will spend their lives plotting revenge, which will always cost you more in the long run than a decisive, complete victory.
