# Hell Yes or No Filter

The Hell Yes or No Filter is a selection heuristic stating that unless an option or opportunity triggers an enthusiastic, unambiguous "Hell Yes!", the default response must be an absolute "No."

## Explanation

Originating from Greg McKeown’s book *Essentialism*, this filter prevents individuals from cluttering their lives and careers with low-value opportunities. Most people say "yes" to career options or projects simply because they seem interesting, easy, or safe. This low bar quickly disperses their focus across too many directions.

By enforcing a high bar, the filter dictates that if you do not feel a strong, intrinsic pull toward an option, it deserves a "no." This preserves critical focus and cognitive bandwidth, ensuring that when you finally encounter a "Hell Yes" opportunity, you have the resources to pursue it with complete commitment.

## Why It Matters

Using this filter resolves career confusion by drastically reducing the number of active options you permit into your life, forcing you to focus only on highly aligned pursuits.
