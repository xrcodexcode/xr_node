# Bayesian Decision Making

Assign probabilities to outcomes, update beliefs as new evidence arrives, and make decisions based on expected value rather than binary thinking.

## Explanation

Musk frames decisions probabilistically, not as "will this work or not?" but as "what is the probability distribution of outcomes, and what is the expected value?" This allows him to pursue low-probability, high-impact bets that look irrational to binary thinkers.

Starting SpaceX: Musk assigned maybe ~10% probability of success. But the expected value of a successful commercial space company was enormous, making the bet rational even with high failure probability.

He applies Bayesian updating: as SpaceX achieved successes (Falcon 1 reaching orbit, Falcon 9 landing), he updated his probability estimates upward, greenlighting more ambitious projects (Starship, Starlink).

## Why it matters

Bayesian thinking enables rational risk-taking. Most people reject a 10% chance of success. A Bayesian sees the expected value. It also prevents stubbornness: evidence genuinely changes Musk's estimates — he does not hold fixed beliefs in the face of contrary data.
