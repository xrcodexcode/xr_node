# Warren Buffett's Delegation Model

## Core idea
Warren Buffett practices extreme operational delegation, giving the managers of Berkshire Hathaway's subsidiaries near-total autonomy over their business decisions, while retaining centralized control strictly over capital allocation.

## Why it matters / connection
Most large conglomerates suffer from corporate bureaucracy, where decision-making is slowed down by multiple layers of management and reporting. Berkshire operates with a tiny headquarters (only about 25 people in Omaha) for a company with hundreds of thousands of employees. Buffett trusts his managers completely, telling them to operate as if they own 100% of their businesses. This delegation model attracts high-quality business owners who want to sell to Berkshire but continue running their companies without interference. centralizing only the capital allocation decisions ensures that surplus cash from all subsidiaries is funneled to the most profitable investment opportunities globally, rather than being trapped in low-return operations.
