# Contrast Effect Bias

## Core idea
Contrast Effect Bias is the cognitive bias where our evaluation of an object's quality or value is significantly enhanced or diminished when compared directly to a recently observed object or standard.

## Why it matters / connection
In corporate acquisitions, buyers often overpay for a business because its price seems cheap compared to a previous bubble valuation, rather than evaluating its absolute intrinsic value. Buffett prevents this by evaluating companies against absolute discount rates and long-term US Treasury yields.
