# Otto von Bismarck and Realpolitik

## Core idea
Otto von Bismarck, the architect of unified Germany, is the ultimate exemplar of Realpolitik—politics based on practical, material objectives and ruthless calculation rather than moral or ideological principles.

## Why it matters / connection
Bismarck manipulated alliances and engineered wars with Denmark, Austria, and France to consolidate German states under Prussian leadership. He made himself indispensable to the weak King William I, frequently using the threat of resignation to get his way. He understood when to crush enemies completely (such as France in 1871) and when to show moderation to preserve a future balance of power (such as Austria in 1866), demonstrating absolute strategic flexibility.
