# Neuralink Mission

A brain-computer interface company founded in 2016 to create high-bandwidth communication between human brains and computers, primarily to mitigate AI risk.

## Explanation

Neuralink develops implantable brain-machine interfaces (BMIs) — tiny electrode threads thinner than a human hair implanted into the brain by a surgical robot. The immediate medical goal: treat neurological conditions (paralysis, blindness, memory loss). The long-term goal: create symbiosis between human intelligence and AI.

Musk's reasoning: if humans cannot communicate faster than verbal speech (limited to ~10 bits/second), AI will vastly outpace human cognition. A brain-computer interface could increase human-AI bandwidth by orders of magnitude, giving humanity a "fighting chance" against superintelligent AI.
