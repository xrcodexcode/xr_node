# Phelps and El Guerrouj

## Core idea
Michael Phelps and Hicham El Guerrouj are both legendary, world-class athletes, but they possess vastly different physical builds. Michael Phelps is a swimmer who stands at six feet, four inches, with a long torso and relatively short legs. Hicham El Guerrouj is a Moroccan runner who is five feet, nine inches, with a short torso and incredibly long legs. Despite their seven-inch height difference, both men wear the same length inseam on their pants. 

This difference in body proportions represents a genetic optimization for their respective sports. Phelps' long torso and short legs are ideal for pulling through the water, while El Guerrouj's long legs and light upper body are perfect for distance running. If they were to switch sports, neither would succeed at the elite level: Phelps at 194 pounds would be too heavy for distance running (where extra weight is a detriment), and El Guerrouj would lack the physical frame (long back and arms) to qualify as an Olympic-caliber swimmer.

## Why it matters / connection
This example demonstrates that genes do not operate in a vacuum; rather, environment determines the suitability and utility of our natural talents. To maximize the odds of success, one must choose the right field of competition. Habits are easier to build and maintain when they align with your natural inclinations and biological advantages.
