# Arbitrage and Workout Investments

## Core idea
Workout Investments (or Arbitrage) involve capital allocation to announced corporate events—such as mergers, acquisitions, liquidations, or reorganizations—where payouts depend on event completion rather than market fluctuations.

## Why it matters / connection
During flat or declining markets, Buffett allocated partnership capital to workouts to achieve stable, uncorrelated returns. This strategy requires calculating the probability of the transaction closing, the time delay, and the opportunity cost compared to holding cash.
