# Make Other People Come to You — Use Bait if Necessary

## Core idea
When you force the other person to act, you are the one in control. It is always better to make your opponent come to you, abandoning his own plans in the process. Lure him with fabulous gains—then attack. You hold the cards.

## Why it matters / connection
Napoleon was baited by Talleyrand into leaving his exile on Elba and returning to France. Talleyrand knew Napoleon's aggressive ambition would lead him to overextend and ultimately destroy himself, which happened at Waterloo. In business, J.P. Morgan would make rival financiers come to his office, forcing them onto his home turf where they were uncomfortable and ready to capitulate.
