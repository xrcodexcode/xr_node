# Delete-Then-Optimize Loop

Never optimize something you should delete. First remove unnecessary parts, then simplify, then accelerate, then automate — in that order.

## Explanation

Musk's engineering process follows a strict sequence that he calls the "Algorithm" — a five-step method for efficient engineering:

1. **Question every requirement** — requirements from smart people may still be wrong
2. **Delete any part or process you can** — if you don't add it back at least 10% of the time, you didn't delete enough
3. **Simplify and optimize** — only after deletion, never before
4. **Accelerate** — speed up the remaining processes
5. **Automate** — the last step, not the first

The critical insight: steps 3-5 are useless if applied to something that shouldn't exist. Musk observed most engineering teams optimize first and ask whether to delete later — backwards.

## Why it matters

This directly counters the common engineering trap of automating or optimizing a fundamentally flawed or unnecessary process. Tesla and SpaceX use this sequence for manufacturing line design, supply chain decisions, and product development.
