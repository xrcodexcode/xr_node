# Mars Colonization Mission

The single overarching goal driving all of Musk's decisions: establish a self-sustaining human city on Mars within his lifetime. Everything else is subordinate.

## Explanation

Musk's core motivation for SpaceX (and by extension much of his wealth) is making humanity multi-planetary. His reasoning: civilization on Earth faces existential risks (AI, nuclear war, asteroid impact, pandemic, climate change). A self-sustaining Mars colony ensures life as a whole survives any single-planet catastrophe.

The technical path: Starship (fully reusable launch vehicle) → Mars transit (6-9 months) → in-situ resource utilization (make fuel from Martian atmosphere) → build city (starting with ~100 people, scaling to 1M).

Musk has said: "I want to die on Mars. Just not on impact."

## Why it matters

This is the mission that attracts top talent to SpaceX, justifies the Starship architecture, and frames his 50-year time horizon. Every major SpaceX decision (reusability, Raptor engine, Starlink revenue) serves this ultimate goal.
