# Special Situations Investing

## Core idea
Special Situations Investing is a value strategy targeting temporary, non-recurring corporate disruptions—such as spin-offs, regulatory shifts, lawsuits, or management changes—that depress asset prices below their intrinsic value.

## Why it matters / connection
These situations create structural mispricings because institutional investors are forced to sell off assets due to mandate limits rather than economic logic. Buffett capitalized on these anomalies to secure high margin-of-safety entries.
