# Return on Equity Metric

## Core idea
Return on Equity (ROE) is a measure of a corporation's profitability relative to stockholder equity, representing the efficiency with which a firm uses shareholder capital to generate profits.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett prefers evaluating management using ROE rather than Earnings Per Share (EPS), as companies can increase EPS simply by retaining earnings and expanding their asset base. A high and stable ROE indicates a strong competitive advantage and efficient management of retained capital.
