# Habits Plus Deliberate Practice

## Core idea
The formula for elite performance and mastery is:
$$\text{Habits} + \text{Deliberate Practice} = \text{Mastery}$$

While habits are the foundation of any pursuit of excellence (automating basic actions like piece movements in chess or initial incisions in surgery to free up cognitive space for higher-level thinking), they also come with a significant cost:
* **The Downside of Habits**: As behaviors become automatic, we become less sensitive to feedback. We fall into mindless repetition, allow minor errors to slide, and stop looking for ways to improve. Research shows that once a skill is mastered, there is often a slight decline in performance over time because we reinforce current habits rather than improving them.
* **The Master's Cycle**: True mastery requires narrowing your focus to one element of success, repeating it until it is internalized as an automatic habit, and then returning to effortful thinking to layer the next challenge.

To maintain elite performance, one must avoid the trap of complacency by remaining conscious of their execution over time.

## Why it matters / connection
This concept reframes the ultimate goal of behavior change: habits are not the destination, but the starting point. It warns against the "good enough" autopilot trap that stalls growth. To continually progress, habits must be paired with conscious, structured feedback, which is achieved through [[reflection-and-review]] systems. It connects directly to the progressive layering of the [[goldilocks-rule]].
