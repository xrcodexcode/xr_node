# Value Investing Philosophy

## Core idea
Value Investing is an investment paradigm that involves buying securities at a market price significantly below their calculated intrinsic value. It treats a stock not as a trading vehicle, but as a fractional interest in a real enterprise.

## Why it matters / connection
This philosophy, formalized by Benjamin Graham and David Dodd, forms the cornerstone of Warren Buffett's wealth. Instead of trying to predict short-term stock price movements (speculation), a value investor focuses on business fundamentals, balance sheets, and cash flows. By viewing volatility as an ally ("Mr. Market" offering bargains) rather than a risk, value investors can purchase assets at discounts, creating a margin of safety that protects capital while maximizing long-term returns.
