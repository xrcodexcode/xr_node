# Win Through Your Actions, Never Through Argument

## Core idea
Any momentary triumph you think you have won through argument is really a Pyrrhic victory: The resentment and ill will you stir up is stronger and lasts longer than any momentary change of opinion. It is much more powerful to get others to agree with you through your actions, without saying a word. Demonstrate, do not explicate.

## Why it matters / connection
When a Roman consul requested a cedar mast for a siege engine, a military engineer argued that a pine mast would be far superior. The consul was so infuriated by the engineer's insolent arguing that he had him whipped, despite the engineer being technically correct. In contrast, Michelangelo won a dispute with the mayor of Florence over the nose of David by pretending to chisel it while letting dust fall, satisfying the mayor without changing the sculpture.
