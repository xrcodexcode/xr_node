# Elon Musk Zip2

Musk's first company: an online city guide software provider co-founded with brother Kimbal in 1995. Sold to Compaq for $307 million in 1999.

## Explanation

Zip2 was founded as Global Link Information Network in Palo Alto in 1995. The idea came from a summer internship where Musk saw a Yellow Pages salesman pitch online business listings. The company provided software for newspapers to create online city guides with maps, business directories, and directions — a radical idea in 1995.

Musk and Kimbal slept in the office and showered at the YMCA. Musk did all the coding initially. The company raised ~$4M from angel investors and won contracts with The New York Times, The Chicago Tribune, and other major newspapers.

Compaq acquired Zip2 in 1999 for $307 million in cash. Musk, then 27, received ~$22 million from the sale.

## Key facts

- Founded: 1995 (as Global Link Information Network)
- Co-founders: Elon Musk, Kimbal Musk, Greg Kouri
- Acquired: 1999 by Compaq for $307M
- Musk's share: ~$22M
- Employees lived at the office during early days

## Why it matters

Zip2 taught Musk the startup playbook: code the product, sleep at the office, raise money from angels, sell to a larger company. It gave him capital for his next ventures and proved he could build and sell a company.
