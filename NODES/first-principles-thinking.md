# First Principles Thinking

Break problems down to their fundamental truths, then reason upward from there rather than reasoning by analogy.

## Explanation

First principles thinking is the practice of actively questioning every assumption until you reach the most basic, undeniable truths — the "first principles" — and then building your solution up from those truths. Musk learned this method from physics, as opposed to the "reasoning by analogy" taught in business school.

Musk's canonical example: when told battery packs cost $600/kWh, he broke the battery into constituent materials (cobalt, nickel, aluminum, carbon, polymers). At commodity market prices, the raw materials cost ~$80/kWh. The remaining $520 was manufacturing structure, supply chain margin, and industry convention. This calculation motivated the Gigafactory strategy.

The method has three steps:
1. Identify and challenge assumptions
2. Break down to fundamental elements
3. Rebuild from the ground up

## Why it matters

This is Musk's core cognitive advantage. It allows him to see cost reductions and innovations that the rest of an industry considers impossible. Every major Musk breakthrough — reusable rockets, vertical integration, in-house manufacturing — traces back to a first principles analysis.
