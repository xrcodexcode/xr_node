# Think as You Like but Behave Like Others

## Core idea
If you make a show of going against the times, flaunting your unconventional ideas and unorthodox ways, people will think that you only want attention and that you look down upon them. They will find a way to punish you for making them feel inferior. It is far safer to blend in and nurture the common touch. Share your originality only with tolerant friends and those who are sure to appreciate your uniqueness.

## Why it matters / connection
The Renaissance philosopher Tommaso Campanella spent years writing radical political and theological works. When arrested by the Inquisition, he feigned madness to escape torture and execution, pretending to agree with his captors' beliefs. He survived to write his books in secret, whereas Galileo's public defiance of the Church earned him permanent house arrest.
