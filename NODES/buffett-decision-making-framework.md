# Warren Buffett's Decision-Making Framework

## Core idea
Warren Buffett's decision-making framework is built on extreme filters: minimizing activity, using opportunity cost as the primary hurdle rate, and sorting choices into three piles: "In," "Out," and "Too Hard."

## Why it matters / connection
Most investors fail because they feel compelled to act constantly, leading to emotional, low-conviction decisions. Buffett operates with a "no called strikes" rule—he only swings when a business enters his circle of competence at an undeniable margin of safety. His framework uses opportunity cost to evaluate new options against his best existing investment, raising the bar for any new acquisition. By immediately routing complex or uncertain situations to the "too hard" pile, he avoids cognitive strain and saves valuable analytical energy for high-conviction decisions.
