# Court Power

## Core idea
The 'court' is the physical or social arena where power is concentrated and exercised. In any system—whether a royal palace, a modern corporate office, or a social circle—power revolves around a central figure (the king, the CEO, the leader). Courtiers must navigate this environment using grace, charm, and calculation rather than overt force.

## Why it matters / connection
Throughout history, power has always structured itself as a court. The courtier's goal is to gain the master's favor while neutralizing rivals. Overt aggression is punished at court; instead, the courtier uses indirection, subtle flattery, and yielding. Versailles under Louis XIV is the historical peak of court power, where every daily detail was a move in the game of status.
