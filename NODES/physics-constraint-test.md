# Physics Constraint Test

Before evaluating economic or organizational feasibility, ask: is this physically possible? The laws of physics are the binding constraint. Everything else is negotiable.

## Explanation

Musk runs every idea through a physics filter first. If physics says it's possible, then engineering and economic feasibility are solvable problems. If physics says it's impossible, no amount of money or effort changes that.

The canonical example: Musk concluded flying cars (as depicted in Back to the Future) are a bad idea because physics says the noise from rotor downwash would be deafening and the energy requirements make them impractical. He moved on without further analysis.

## Why it matters

This filter prevents wasted resources on projects that are fundamentally impossible. It also reframes "impossible" problems: many things called "impossible" are merely economically infeasible given current approaches — a solvable constraint.
