# Net Operating Loss Tax Shield

## Core idea
A Net Operating Loss (NOL) Tax Shield is an accounting and tax provision that allows businesses to carry forward operating losses to offset future taxable income, reducing corporate tax liability.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett utilized NOLs strategically when purchasing struggling firms (like textile mills or retail operations). By combining these loss-making entities under Berkshire Hathaway's corporate umbrella, he used their tax shields to protect cash generated by Berkshire's highly profitable subsidiaries, maximizing cash for reallocation.
