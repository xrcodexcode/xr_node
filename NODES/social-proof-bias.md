# Social Proof in Investing

## Core idea
Social Proof is the cognitive bias where individuals mimic the actions of the crowd in ambiguous situations, assuming that the group possesses superior information.

## Why it matters / connection
In stock markets, social proof drives herd behavior, leading investors to buy into speculative bubbles at peak prices and panic-sell during corrections. Buffett counters this by adopting contrarian views, arguing that you are neither right nor wrong because the crowd disagrees with you, but because your data is right.
