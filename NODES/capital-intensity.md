# Low Capital Intensity

## Core idea
Low Capital Intensity refers to a business model that requires minimal capital investment in fixed assets (such as machinery or factories) to sustain operations and grow revenues.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett prefers asset-light businesses because they generate high free cash flows that can be diverted to other high-return opportunities. High capital-intensity businesses (like manufacturing or airlines) must reinvest most of their earnings just to maintain current production, hampering compounding.
