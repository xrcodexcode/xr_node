# Marmon Group Acquisition

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway acquired Marmon Group, a diverse industrial conglomerate with over 100 manufacturing and service companies, in stages starting in 2008 for $4.5 billion, completing the 100% acquisition in 2013. The company was previously owned by the Pritzker family.

## Why it matters / connection
Marmon Group is a classic example of Berkshire's acquisition philosophy: buying a large, diversified, well-managed conglomerate with low capital intensity and strong cash flows, then allowing it to run with absolute operational autonomy. It serves as a capital provider for Berkshire's broader reinvestment pool.
