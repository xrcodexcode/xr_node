# Coca-Cola Investment Case Study

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway's purchase of $1.3 billion of Coca-Cola stock between 1988 and 1994 represents the ultimate brand equity investment, exploiting global brand power and market pricing power to capture a compounding stream of dividend growth.

## Why it matters / connection
Following the 1987 stock market crash, Coca-Cola's stock price fell significantly. Buffett calculated that Coca-Cola's global dominance, product simplicity, and brand equity formed an indestructible economic moat. Since the purchase, Berkshire has not sold a single share of its Coca-Cola holding. Because Coca-Cola's profit margins sustained growth and they raised their dividend yearly, Berkshire's annual dividend cash flow from Coca-Cola rose from $75 million in 1995 to over $700+ million today. This demonstrates how purchasing a massive brand moat at a discount yields a compounding return far outstripping the initial purchase price.
