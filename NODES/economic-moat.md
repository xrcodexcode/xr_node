# Concept of the Economic Moat

## Core idea
An Economic Moat is a business's sustainable competitive advantage that protects its high returns on capital and market share from competitor erosion, similar to a moat protecting a castle.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett popularized this term because high profit margins naturally attract competitors, who drive margins down in a commodity market. A business with an economic moat can sustain high profits because of structural barriers, such as:
1. **Brand Power / Pricing Power**: Customers buy the product even if prices rise (e.g. Coca-Cola, See's Candies).
2. **High Switching Costs**: Making it expensive or complex for customers to switch to a competitor (e.g. enterprise software).
3. **Network Effects**: The product's value increases as more people use it (e.g. American Express).
4. **Cost Advantage**: Producing goods or services at a lower cost than rivals (e.g. GEICO's direct-to-consumer distribution model).
Evaluating the strength and durability of a business's moat is crucial for calculating its intrinsic value.
