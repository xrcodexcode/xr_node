# General Reinsurance Acquisition

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway acquired General Reinsurance (Gen Re), a dominant global reinsurance company, in 1998 for $22 billion. The transaction was paid entirely in Berkshire Hathaway stock, which expanded Berkshire's capital base.

## Why it matters / connection
Gen Re significantly expanded Berkshire's insurance float, providing low-cost capital for Buffett's equity investments. Although the acquisition initially suffered from underwriting losses and required strict operational cleanup, it eventually became a highly profitable pillar of Berkshire's insurance network.
