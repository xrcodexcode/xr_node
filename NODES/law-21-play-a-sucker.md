# Play a Sucker to Catch a Sucker — Seem Dumber Than Your Mark

## Core idea
No one likes feeling stupider than the next person. The trick, then, is to make your victims feel smart—and not just smart, but smarter than you are. Once convinced of this, they will never suspect that you may have ulterior motives, and they will let down their guard.

## Why it matters / connection
When con artists swindled Andrew Mellon in the 'Great Diamond Hoax,' they pretended to be simple, uneducated prospectors who had accidentally found a massive diamond mine. Mellon, a sophisticated and wealthy financier, assumed he could easily outsmart them and buy the mine cheaply. His intellectual arrogance made him blind to the scam, and he lost hundreds of thousands of dollars.
