# Elon Musk Tesla Founding

Musk did not found Tesla. He joined as lead investor and Chairman of the Board in 2004, then became CEO during the 2008 financial crisis. He is the driving force behind Tesla's strategy and products.

## Explanation

Tesla was founded in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Musk led the Series A round in 2004, investing $7.5M (most of his PayPal proceeds) and becoming Chairman. He was deeply involved in product design, particularly the Roadster.

By 2007-2008, tensions between Musk and Eberhard led to Eberhard's departure, and Musk took operational control. During the 2008 financial crisis, Tesla was weeks from bankruptcy. Musk invested his last $20M, personally signed loan guarantees, and moved Tesla to survival. He became formal CEO in October 2008.

Tesla's milestones under Musk: Roadster (2008), Model S (2012), Model X (2015), Model 3 (2017), Model Y (2020), Cybertruck (2023). The company became the most valuable automaker by market cap in 2020.

## Key facts

- Joined: 2004 (lead investor, Chairman)
- Became CEO: October 2008
- Initial investment: $7.5M
- Saved Tesla in 2008 with personal capital
- Tesla IPO: June 2010, raised $226M

## Why it matters

Musk transformed Tesla from a startup making a niche sports car into the highest-value automaker in the world. His first-principles approach to battery costs, vertical integration strategy, and manufacturing philosophy (the Algorithm) directly produced this outcome.
