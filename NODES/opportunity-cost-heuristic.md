# Opportunity Cost Heuristic

## Core idea
Opportunity Cost is the value of the next best alternative that is foregone when making a decision. In Warren Buffett's framework, opportunity cost is the primary "hurdle rate" used to judge all new investments.

## Why it matters / connection
Traditional finance compares new investments to theoretical discount rates or cost of capital models. Buffett and Munger reject this, instead comparing any new proposal directly to their best existing opportunity. If a new business acquisition does not offer higher returns than buying more shares of Berkshire's best existing holding (such as Apple or Coca-Cola) at current prices, it is immediately discarded. This simple heuristic filters out mediocre opportunities, keeping capital concentrated in a small number of high-quality assets.
