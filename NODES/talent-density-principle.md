# Talent Density Principle

One exceptional engineer is worth more than ten average ones. Build small, elite teams of outstanding people rather than large teams of competent ones.

## Explanation

Musk believes that talent density — the concentration of exceptional ability in a team — is the primary determinant of engineering output. He famously said at SpaceX: "The best person is worth 10x, maybe 100x the average person."

This manifests in practice:
- SpaceX and Tesla keep engineering teams small relative to their output
- Musk personally interviews every early engineer
- He fires low performers quickly to preserve density
- At Twitter/X, he reduced headcount from 7,900 to 1,500 while maintaining output

## Why it matters

Talent density enables speed, reduces coordination overhead, and increases the probability of breakthroughs. A small team of exceptional people can outproduce a large team of average ones because communication paths grow quadratically with team size.
