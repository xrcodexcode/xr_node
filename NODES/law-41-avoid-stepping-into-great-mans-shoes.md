# Avoid Stepping into a Great Man's Shoes

## Core idea
What happens first always appears better and more original than what comes after. If you succeed a great man or have a famous parent, you will have to double their achievements to outshine them. Do not get lost in their shadow, or stuck in a past not of your own making: Establish your own name and identity by changing course. Slay the overbearing father, disparage his legacy, and gain power by shining in your own way.

## Why it matters / connection
King Louis XV of France lived in the shadow of his great-grandfather Louis XIV. Instead of defining a new direction for the country, he tried to maintain the Sun King's legacy, leading to comparison, stagnation, and public contempt. Alexander the Great's successors similarly failed because they fought to preserve his empire rather than building their own states.
