# Two-Step Process to Identity Change

## Core idea
Identity is learned and conditioned through experience. The word *identity* is derived from the Latin words *essentitas* ("being") and *identidem* ("repeatedly"), translating literally to "repeated beingness." Habits are the primary way we embody our identity; each time we repeat a behavior, we provide evidence that reinforces the identity associated with that behavior.

To change who you are, follow this simple two-step process:
1. **Decide the type of person you want to be**: Define your principles, values, and what you stand for. If you only know the results you want, work backward. Ask: "Who is the type of person that could get the outcome I want?" (e.g., Who could write a book? A consistent and reliable person).
2. **Prove it to yourself with small wins**: Accumulate evidence through small habits. Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become. You do not need to be perfect or have a unanimous vote to win an election; you just need a majority of votes over time.

## Why it matters / connection
This framework explains how self-image is built. We cannot simply manifest a new identity by willpower or positive thinking; we need physical evidence. By focusing on small wins rather than overnight transformations, we make habit change sustainable and practical. It highlights that the most practical way to change who you are is to change what you do.
