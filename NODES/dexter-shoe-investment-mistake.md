# Dexter Shoe Acquisition Mistake

## Core idea
Berkshire acquired Dexter Shoe Company in 1993 for $433 million. Paying for the transaction entirely with Berkshire Hathaway stock, Buffett later called this the worst investment decision of his career.

## Why it matters / connection
Dexter's competitive advantage (economic moat) as a domestic manufacturer was destroyed by low-cost foreign competition. Furthermore, using Berkshire stock (which compounded in value) to buy a business that went to zero meant the mistake cost shareholders billions of dollars, highlighting the danger of misjudging moats.
