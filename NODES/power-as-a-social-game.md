# Power as an Amoral Social Game

## Core idea
The central thesis of Robert Greene's work is that power is an ancient, amoral social game. Refusing to play the game does not protect you; it only means you play it poorly and become a victim to those who understand its rules.

## Why it matters / connection
Greene argues that human society has always been structured around hierarchies, status, and control. The 48 Laws are not moral guidelines, but observations of how power actually operates. To thrive, one must view human interactions objectively, master their own emotions, and learn to decode the subtle strategies of others, treating power as a skill to be mastered rather than a moral debate.
