# Charlie Munger's Influence on Warren Buffett

## Core idea
Charlie Munger, the Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway, shifted Warren Buffett's investment philosophy away from Benjamin Graham's strict quantitative bargain hunting (buying low-quality companies at deep discounts) toward buying high-quality businesses with strong economic moats at fair prices.

## Why it matters / connection
Graham's traditional value strategy focused on "cigar butts"—companies that had one puff left in them but were extremely cheap. As Berkshire grew, this approach became unscalable. Munger convinced Buffett that "it's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." This intellectual transition allowed Berkshire to scale its capital allocation, leading to major successful acquisitions like See's Candies, GEICO, and Apple, and forming the modern Berkshire Hathaway business model.
