# Berkshire Hathaway Acquisition

## Core idea
Warren Buffett's acquisition of Berkshire Hathaway, a declining New England textile company, in 1965 was initially an emotional reaction that he later called his "most monumental mistake," but it eventually evolved into his central diversified holding company.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett originally bought shares of Berkshire Hathaway because the management offered to buy back his shares at a set price, but then lowered the offer by 12.5 cents. Incensed by this, Buffett bought control of the company to fire the CEO. This emotional decision tied up significant capital in a declining domestic textile industry. Recognizing the textile business could not be saved, Buffett pivoted by using Berkshire's cash flow to purchase insurance companies (National Indemnity, and later GEICO). This provided Berkshire with a source of low-cost capital ("insurance float") to purchase high-quality businesses, turning a failing textile company into a multi-billion-dollar compounding vehicle.
