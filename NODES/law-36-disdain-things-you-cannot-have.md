# Disdain Things You Cannot Have — Ignoring Them is the Best Revenge

## Core idea
By acknowledging a petty problem you give it existence and credibility. The more attention you pay an enemy, the stronger you make him; and a tiny mistake is often made worse and more visible when you try to fix it. It is sometimes best to leave things alone. If there is something you want but cannot have, show contempt for it. The less interest you reveal, the more superior you seem.

## Why it matters / connection
When a minor rebellion broke out against Mexican President Porfirio Díaz, he refused to send troops or make public statements. By ignoring it, he denied the rebels the status of a legitimate threat, causing their movement to lose momentum and disappear. Acknowledge an opponent and you elevate them to your level; disdain them and you leave them impotent.
