# Court Attention at All Costs

## Core idea
Everything is judged by its appearance; what is unseen counts for nothing. Never let yourself get lost in the crowd, then, or buried in oblivion. Stand out. Be conspicuous, at all cost. Make yourself a magnet of attention by appearing larger, more colorful, more mysterious than the bland and timid masses.

## Why it matters / connection
P.T. Barnum courted attention by printing sensational stories, staging elaborate fake exhibits, and starting rumors about his own attractions. If people were talking about him, it didn't matter if it was positive or negative; attention translated directly to ticket sales. Similarly, artists like Thomas Chatterton created a sense of mystery around their work by pretending to discover ancient manuscripts, which fascinated the public and elevated their status.
