# Preach the Need for Change, but Never Reform Too Much at Once

## Core idea
Everyone understands the need for change in the abstract, but on the daily level humans are creatures of habit. Too much innovation is traumatic, and will lead to revolt. If you are new to a position of power, or an outsider trying to build a base, make a show of respecting the old way of doing things. If change is necessary, make it feel like a gentle improvement of the past.

## Why it matters / connection
Thomas Cromwell reformed the English Church too quickly, abolishing monasteries and traditional holidays overnight. This created massive rebellions (like the Pilgrimage of Grace) that ultimately cost him the King's favor and his head. Machiavelli advised that a new ruler should keep the outward forms and rituals of the old regime, changing the reality behind them slowly.
