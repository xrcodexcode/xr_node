# Delete Before Optimize

Never optimize a process, part, or requirement that you should delete. The biggest efficiency gain is removing what shouldn't exist.

## Explanation

This is the distilled form of steps 2-3 of Musk's Algorithm. The critical insight is that optimization makes waste more efficient. If a process adds no value, making it run faster or cheaper still produces zero value.

Musk's test: if you delete something and you don't add it back at least 10% of the time, you didn't delete enough. This forces aggressive removal because most teams hesitate to delete.

At Tesla, when designing the Model 3 production line, Musk found a section of the line dedicated to applying a certain fabric. No one knew why it was there. It was deleted. The car was built without it. No one noticed.

## Why it matters

Most organizations optimize first and ask "should this exist?" after — the wrong order. Deleting first produces step-function cost reductions and simplifications that optimization alone could never achieve.
