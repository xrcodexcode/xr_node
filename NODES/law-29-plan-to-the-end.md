# Plan All the Way to the End

## Core idea
The ending is everything. Plan all the way to it, taking into account all the possible consequences, obstacles, and twists of fortune that might reverse your hard work and give the glory to others. By planning to the end you will not be overwhelmed by circumstances and you will know when to stop. Gently guide fortune and help determine the future by thinking far ahead.

## Why it matters / connection
Otto von Bismarck planned the unification of Germany with incredible foresight. He knew he needed to isolate Austria and defeat France, but he also knew when to stop: After defeating Austria in 1866, he refused to annex Austrian territory, preserving them as a future ally. Conversely, Napoleon failed to plan past his initial victories, leading him to invade Russia and exhaust his empire.
