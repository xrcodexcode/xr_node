# Strategic Deception

## Core idea
In Robert Greene's philosophy, deception is not an immoral aberration but a fundamental law of nature and human interaction. Borrowing from Sun Tzu's maxim that 'all warfare is based on deception,' strategic deception is the art of misdirecting attention, concealing intentions, and letting others deceive themselves.

## Why it matters / connection
Strategic deception involves creating smoke screens (such as false sincerity, routine behavior, or selective honesty) to throw rivals off the scent. It is not about lying constantly, which creates suspicion; it is about managing information and appearances so that others act on false assumptions. By controlling what others see and believe, you control their subsequent actions.
