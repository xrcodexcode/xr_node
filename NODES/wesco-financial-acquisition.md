# Wesco Financial

## Core idea
Wesco Financial Corporation was a savings and loan association that Berkshire Hathaway acquired control of in the 1970s. It was managed for decades by Charlie Munger, who served as its CEO.

## Why it matters / connection
Wesco was operated as a mini-conglomerate, applying the same value investing and capital allocation principles as Berkshire but on a smaller scale. Munger used its cash reserves to acquire subsidiaries (like CORT Furniture) and purchase stocks, serving as a key capital allocator.
