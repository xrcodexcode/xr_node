# Re-Create Yourself

## Core idea
Do not accept the roles that society foists on you. Re-create yourself by forging a new identity, one that commands attention and never bores the audience. Be the master of your own image rather than letting others define it for you. Incorporate dramatic devices into your public gestures and actions—your power will be enhanced and your character will seem larger than life.

## Why it matters / connection
Julius Caesar was a master of self-recreation. He designed spectacular public games, wrote third-person accounts of his military achievements, and rehearsed dramatic gestures and pauses in his speeches to project an image of a heroic leader. Similarly, George Sand (born Aurore Dupin) wore male attire and adopted a male pseudonym to escape the limitations placed on female writers in nineteenth-century France.
