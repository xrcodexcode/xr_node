# Poison of Optionality

The Poison of Optionality is the principle that keeping multiple options open generates a false sense of security while actively preventing a person from committing fully to any single path.

## Explanation

While "keeping options open" is common career advice, maintaining too many parallel paths without a stable anchor breeds confusion and paralysis. True diversification requires at least one committed baseline. If a person attempts to split focus across multiple unanchored goals (e.g., preparing for competitive exams, working, and starting a business simultaneously), none receive the energy required to succeed.

Optionality acts as a psychological poison because the mere illusion of better alternatives prevents complete commitment. For example, keeping dating apps active during a relationship dilutes focus on the partner, just as keeping back-up jobs open dilutes focus on a current career path.

## Why It Matters

Recognizing optionality as a form of self-sabotage allows individuals to intentionally burn bridges and close off distraction channels. True progress requires closing secondary doors to allocate 100% of cognitive resources to a single, chosen direction.
