# Environment Priming

## Core idea
Environment priming is the method of organizing and structuring a physical space to make the next intended action frictionless and easy, or to make an unwanted behavior highly difficult. Instead of just cleaning up after a past action, you prepare the environment for future use, a strategy developer Oswald Nuckols calls **"resetting the room"** (e.g., folding the blanket and placing the remote on the TV stand after watching TV so it is ready for the next session).

Examples of environment priming include:
- **To eat a healthy breakfast**: Place the skillet on the stove, cooking spray on the counter, and utensils out the night before.
- **To practice drawing**: Place notebooks, pens, and drawing tools on top of your desk within easy reach.
- **To exercise**: Lay out workout clothes, running shoes, and water bottle ahead of time.
- **To curb television usage**: Unplug the TV after each use and take the batteries out of the remote so it takes extra effort to turn it back on.
- **To focus**: Place your smartphone in a different room until lunch to increase the friction of checking it mindlessly.

## Why it matters / connection
Priming the environment is a core technique of the 3rd Law of Behavior Change (Make It Easy). It acknowledges that human behavior is heavily influenced by the immediate environment. By reducing the physical and cognitive friction associated with positive habits and increasing the friction for negative ones, you make doing the right thing the path of least resistance.
