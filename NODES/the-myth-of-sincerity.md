# The Myth of Sincerity

## Core idea
Absolute honesty is socially destructive. Believing that sincerity is the best path to trust or connection is a naive illusion; in reality, unchecked truthfulness is often perceived as arrogance, tactlessness, or a lack of self-control.

## Why it matters / connection
Robert Greene argues that total honesty is a weapon of the weak, who use it to avoid the effort of self-control and calculated presentation. In a social arena, people want to hear comfortable lies, fantasies, and flattery. Revealing your raw thoughts makes you predictable, vulnerable, and offensive. Master strategists treat sincerity as a resource—applying it selectively and theatrically to disarm others, while concealing their true motives behind a polite, socially acceptable exterior.
