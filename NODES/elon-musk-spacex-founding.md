# Elon Musk SpaceX Founding

Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies (SpaceX) in 2002 with $100M of his PayPal proceeds. The mission: make humanity multi-planetary by reducing launch costs 100x.

## Explanation

Musk's original goal was to send a greenhouse to Mars to inspire public interest in space. When he discovered that Russian rockets cost $20M+ each and US launch prices were similarly prohibitive, he applied [[first-principles-thinking]]: raw rocket materials cost ~2% of the purchase price. The markup was industry convention, not physics. He decided to build rockets himself.

SpaceX's early history was defined by near-failure. The first three Falcon 1 launches failed (2006, 2007, 2008). The company had money for exactly one more launch. Falcon 1 Flight 4 reached orbit on September 28, 2008 — a date Musk calls "the closest I've ever been to broken." The next day, NASA awarded SpaceX a $1.6B Commercial Resupply Services contract.

## Key milestones

- Founded: May 2002
- First launch (Falcon 1): 2006 (failed)
- First orbit (Falcon 1 Flight 4): September 28, 2008
- First Dragon to ISS: May 2012
- First landing of orbital-class booster: December 2015
- First crewed flight (Demo-2): May 2020
- First Starship orbital attempt: 2023

## Why it matters

SpaceX is the first private company to: reach orbit, dock with the ISS, land an orbital rocket, and launch humans to space. It has reduced launch costs by ~10x and is actively developing Starship for Mars colonization. The company is arguably Musk's most important venture because its success determines whether humanity becomes multi-planetary.
