# Do Not Go Past the Mark You Aimed For — In Victory, Learn When to Stop

## Core idea
The moment of victory is often the moment of greatest peril. In the heat of victory, arrogance and overconfidence can push you past the mark you had aimed for, and by going too far, you make more enemies than you defeat. Do not allow success to go to your head. Nothing can substitute for compromise and careful planning. Set a goal, and when you reach it, stop.

## Why it matters / connection
Cyrus the Great conquered the Medes, Lydians, and Babylonians, establishing a massive empire. However, in his arrogance, he pushed further to conquer the Massagetae, ignoring warnings, and was killed in battle. Napoleon similarly failed to stop after his initial conquests, leading to the disastrous invasion of Russia. A strategist knows when victory has been achieved.
