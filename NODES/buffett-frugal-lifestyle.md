# Warren Buffett's Frugality

## Core idea
Warren Buffett's simple, frugal lifestyle is a core element of his personality and business methodology, demonstrating that low personal spending rates prevent financial distractions and preserve capital for compounding.

## Why it matters / connection
Despite having a net worth of over $100 billion, Buffett lives in the same five-bedroom house in Omaha that he purchased in 1958 for $31,500, drives relatively modest cars, and famously eats breakfast at McDonald's for under $4 daily. This lifestyle is not just an eccentric habit; it reflects a deep, fundamental focus on long-term value over short-term consumption. It ensures that his attention remains entirely on business allocation rather than luxury maintenance, serving as a powerful cultural symbol inside Berkshire Hathaway where cost-consciousness is treated as a major corporate value.
