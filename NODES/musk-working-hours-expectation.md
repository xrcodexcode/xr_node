# Leadership by Immersion Expectation

Musk leads by physical immersion — sleeping at factories, working alongside engineers, and expecting the same dedication from his executive team.

## Explanation

Musk's management style includes physically being present at critical operational sites during crises. During Model 3 production hell, he slept in a sleeping bag on the factory floor. During Falcon 1 failures, he was at the launch site. He expects his executives to do the same — being where the hard problems are, not in offices removed from engineering.

He has described his leadership style as "in the factory, doing whatever it takes." This creates a culture where no task is beneath anyone, including the CEO. It also accelerates problem-solving because the decision-maker sees problems firsthand.

## Why it matters

Information degrades through organizational layers. Musk's immersion lets him make decisions based on direct observation rather than filtered reports. It also sets a cultural norm: the leader is not protected from the hardest work.
