# Habit Tracking

## Core idea
Habit tracking is a simple and effective method for measuring whether you stuck to a routine. It visualizes progress through tools like calendars, food logs, workout journals, or physical systems. By measuring actions, habit tracking leverages the Laws of Behavior Change:
1. **It is Obvious**: Tracking creates visual cues (like a chain of X's on a calendar) that prompt the next action. It keeps you honest and overcomes our natural self-blindness; studies show that tracking behaviors like food intake can double weight loss results.
2. **It is Attractive**: The most effective motivator is progress. Seeing visual evidence of your streak creates immediate feedback that drives the urge to keep going and protects motivation on difficult days.
3. **It is Satisfying**: Crossing items off a list or logging an entry is inherently rewarding. It shifts focus from distant outcomes (e.g., getting fit) to the immediate process of keeping the streak alive.

To implement tracking easily, combine it with habit stacking:
* **The Habit Stacking + Habit Tracking Formula**: After `[CURRENT HABIT]`, I will `[TRACK MY HABIT]`.
  * *Example*: "After I hang up the phone from a sales call, I will move one paper clip over."

Additionally, tracking must be supported by the **"never miss twice"** rule to handle inevitable interruptions:
* Missing a habit once is an accident, but missing it twice is the start of a new, negative habit.
* Showing up on bad or busy days—even for a highly scaled-down version of the habit—maintains compound gains and reaffirms your identity.

However, tracking must be kept in check due to **Goodhart's Law**: *"When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."* Ensure you optimize for the actual behavior and identity, not just the number.

## Why it matters / connection
This strategy is a key application of the 4th Law of Behavior Change (Make It Satisfying). It bridges the gap between delayed long-term rewards and immediate gratification, making habits stickier. By requiring action on bad days, it directly feeds into [[identity-based-habits]] by validating the vote for your desired identity even in suboptimal conditions.
