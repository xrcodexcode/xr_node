# Safeguard Soap Handwashing Study

## Core idea
In the late 1990s, public health worker Stephen Luby led an intervention in the squatter settlements of Karachi, Pakistan, where unsanitary conditions caused widespread disease. Luby discovered that while residents knew handwashing was important, they lacked consistency due to the lack of pleasant sensory feedback. 

To solve this, the team partnered with Procter & Gamble to distribute Safeguard soap, a premium soap that lathers easily and smells pleasant. By introducing Safeguard, the handwashing experience became satisfying and pleasurable. The health effects were swift and dramatic: diarrhea rates dropped by 52%, pneumonia by 48%, and skin infections by 35%. A follow-up six years later showed that 95% of the households still maintained a handwashing station with soap and water, having sustained the habit for years without receiving free soap.

## Why it matters / connection
This study demonstrates the **Fourth Law of Behavior Change: Make It Satisfying**. It highlights that consistency in habit adoption is driven by positive sensory feedback and immediate pleasure rather than intellectual knowledge alone.
