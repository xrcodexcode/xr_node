# The Reality of Human Envy

## Core idea
Your success does not inspire others; it breeds silent resentment. Human envy is a natural response to perceived superiority, and those you surpass will look for ways to diminish your achievements and pull you down.

## Why it matters / connection
Greene warns that the primary consequence of success is envy, not admiration. People rarely admit to feeling envious; instead, they disguise it as moral outrage, criticism of your character, or accusations of unfairness. This is why you must never appear too perfect or flaunt your victories. A smart strategist downplays their success, attributes their wins to luck, occasionally displays minor flaws, and strategically yielding to deflect the toxic effects of silent, building resentment.
