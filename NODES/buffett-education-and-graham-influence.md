# Warren Buffett's Education and Benjamin Graham's Influence

## Core idea
Warren Buffett's intellectual foundation was shaped at Columbia Business School under the tutelage of Benjamin Graham, the pioneer of security analysis and value investing, who provided him with a structured framework for analyzing securities.

## Why it matters / connection
Graham taught Buffett to decouple stock prices from business realities, viewing shares as fractional ownership stakes. Graham's model introduced the concepts of "Intrinsic Value" (a firm's true underlying worth) and the "Margin of Safety" (buying below intrinsic value to offset error). This analytical foundation freed Buffett from the emotional fluctuations of the stock market ("Mr. Market"), establishing the core rules of his career: Rule 1: Never lose money; Rule 2: Never forget Rule 1.
