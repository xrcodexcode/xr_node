# Reasoning by Analogy

The default mode of thinking: "we do this because it's like something else that was done." It produces incremental improvement, not breakthrough innovation.

## Explanation

Musk contrasts first principles thinking with "reasoning by analogy" — the dominant mode of human decision-making. Analogy means looking at what others do and copying it with minor modifications. It is efficient (it works most of the time) but produces convergent thinking where every solution looks similar.

Musk's critique: business school teaches reasoning by analogy. Physics teaches reasoning from first principles. The former optimizes within existing paradigms; the latter creates new ones.

## Why it matters

Recognizing when you are reasoning by analogy is the first step to using first principles instead. Most industry "impossibilities" are actually analogical traps: "rockets cost $60M" is an observation about current practice, not a law of physics.
