# Warren Buffett's Reading Habit

## Core idea
Warren Buffett's primary daily method is reading 500+ pages of financial reports, newspapers, and books. He treats reading as a structured habit to build a cumulative information advantage, stating that "knowledge builds up, like compound interest."

## Why it matters / connection
In the information age, most people consume fast, low-quality, real-time news. Buffett filters out this noise by reading primary source documents (annual reports, 10-K filings, historical texts) and spending most of his day in quiet thinking. This extreme reading habit allows him to build a deep, compounding database of industry operations, corporate histories, and business models over decades. This structural knowledge base enables him to make rapid, rational investment decisions when opportunities arise.
