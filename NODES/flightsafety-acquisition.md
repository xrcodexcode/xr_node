# FlightSafety Acquisition

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway acquired FlightSafety International in 1996 for $1.5 billion. Founded by Albert Ueltschi, FlightSafety provides professional simulator-based training to corporate, commercial, and military pilots.

## Why it matters / connection
FlightSafety enjoys an immense economic moat based on switching costs and high capital requirements. Simulators cost millions of dollars, creating a high barrier to entry, while long-term training contracts and strict regulatory requirements lock in corporate aviation clients, generating highly stable, recurring cash flows.
