# Hyperbolic Discounting in Finance

## Core idea
Hyperbolic Discounting is the cognitive bias where humans prefer smaller, immediate rewards over larger, delayed rewards, showing a strong present bias that devalues long-term gains.

## Why it matters / connection
This bias explains why retail investors churn portfolios and Wall Street focuses on quarterly earnings rather than 10-year outlooks. Berkshire Hathaway exploits this by maintaining a permanent holding horizon, allowing compound interest to work undisturbed while others seek short-term liquidity.
