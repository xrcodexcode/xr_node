# Immediate-Return Environment

## Core idea
An Immediate-Return Environment is a setting where actions yield direct, immediate outcomes (such as finding food, avoiding predators, or seeking shelter), which is how the human brain evolved.

## Why it matters / connection
For 99% of human history, humans lived in immediate-return environments. Every decision had immediate consequences: finding berries fed you now, running from a predator saved you now, and sheltering protected you from the immediate storm. Consequently, our brains are neurologically hardwired to prioritize instant gratification over long-term compounding. This creates a severe mismatch in modern delayed-return societies, explaining why we easily fall victim to short-term bad habits that offer immediate pleasure but long-term pain.
