# When Asking for Help, Appeal to Self-Interest, Never to Mercy or Gratitude

## Core idea
If you need to turn to an ally for help, do not bother to remind him of your past assistance and good deeds. He will find a way to ignore you. Instead, uncover something in your request, or in your alliance with him, that will benefit him, and emphasize it out of all proportion. He will respond enthusiastically when he sees something to be gained for himself.

## Why it matters / connection
The Peloponnesian islanders of Corcyra needed help from Athens to defend against Corinth. Rather than appealing to past treaties or moral obligations, they pointed out that their navy, combined with Athens' fleet, would make Athens invincible against Sparta. Athens agreed immediately. On the other hand, the Crotonians appealed to the Sybarites' mercy and gratitude, pointing out past favors, and were ignored and destroyed.
