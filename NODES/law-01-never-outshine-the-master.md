# Never Outshine the Master

## Core idea
Always make those above you feel comfortably superior. In your desire to please or impress them, do not go too far in displaying your talents or you might accomplish the opposite—inspire fear and insecurity. Make your masters appear more brilliant than they are and you will attain the heights of power.

## Why it matters / connection
Nicolas Fouquet, Louis XIV's finance minister, threw an incredibly lavish party at his estate Vaux-le-Vicomte. The party was so grand it upstaged the King, making Louis XIV feel insecure and envious. Within weeks, Fouquet was arrested and spent the rest of his life in prison. In contrast, Galileo Galilei cleverly dedicated his discovery of the moons of Jupiter to the Medici family, representing them as the moons orbiting the cosmic Medici dynasty, thereby securing a lucrative position without threatening their status.
