# Two-Way vs One-Way Doors

Two-Way vs One-Way Doors is a decision-making framework formulated by Jeff Bezos that classifies decisions into reversible (two-way doors) and irreversible (one-way doors) categories to optimize speed and resource allocation.

## Explanation

Bezos splits decisions into two types:
1. **One-Way Doors (Irreversible):** Decisions that are highly consequential and nearly impossible to undo once made. If you step through, the door locks behind you (e.g., quitting a key job, selling a business, or breaking a core relationship). These decisions must be made slowly, carefully, and with extensive deliberation.
2. **Two-Way Doors (Reversible):** Decisions that can be easily undone if the outcome is unfavorable. You can step back through the door at low cost (e.g., subscribing to a class, trying a new software tool, or shifting to a new city temporarily). These decisions should be made quickly by individuals or small teams to maintain velocity.

Many people suffer from career paralysis because they treat two-way doors as one-way doors, wasting excessive mental energy on low-risk, reversible decisions.

## Why It Matters

Using this framework helps individuals overcome choice paralysis. Recognizing that 99% of daily career and life choices are reversible (two-way doors) allows people to act quickly, fail fast, and pivot without carrying emotional or financial ruin.
