# Disarm and Infuriate with the Mirror Effect

## Core idea
The mirror mimics reality, but it is actually a deception. When you mirror your enemies, doing exactly as they do, they cannot figure out your strategy. The Mirror Effect mocks and humiliates them, causing them to overreact. By holding a mirror to their minds, you seduce them with the illusion that you share their values; by holding a mirror to their actions, you teach them a lesson.

## Why it matters / connection
Psychotherapist Milton Erickson would mirror his patients' resistance and speech patterns, which disarmed their defenses and allowed him to guide them. Alcibiades similarly mirrored the customs of whichever city he visited: In Sparta, he was disciplined and athletic; in Persia, he was luxurious and lavish. This mirroring made people trust him instantly.
