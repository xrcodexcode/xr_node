# Sprezzatura

## Core idea
Sprezzatura is an Italian word coined by Baldassare Castiglione in *The Book of the Courtier* (1528). It is defined as a studied carelessness, the art of making extremely difficult actions, social graces, or works of art appear completely effortless and natural.

## Why it matters / connection
In the arena of power, revealing the effort, anxiety, or calculations behind your achievements makes you appear weak and overly ambitious. Sprezzatura is the mask of ease. When a courtier or leader acts with sprezzatura, they project an aura of supreme confidence, competence, and mystery, making others believe their capability is limitless.
