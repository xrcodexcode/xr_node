# Concept of Owner Earnings

## Core idea
Owner Earnings is Warren Buffett's metric for a business's true economic profit, calculated as reported earnings plus depreciation, depletion, and amortization, minus the average annual capital expenditures required to maintain its competitive position.

## Why it matters / connection
Standard accounting metrics (like net income or EBITDA) often distort a company's financial health by ignoring the heavy capital reinvestment needed just to maintain market share. Calculating owner earnings allows investors to determine the actual cash flow available to shareholders and accurately estimate intrinsic value.
