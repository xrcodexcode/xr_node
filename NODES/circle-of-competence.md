# Circle of Competence Heuristic

## Core idea
The Circle of Competence is the boundary of an investor's knowledge and analytical capabilities. Staying inside the circle means only investing in industries, business models, and products that one thoroughly understands, while classifying everything else as "too hard."

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett argues that the size of an investor's circle of competence is far less important than knowing where its perimeter lies. By restricting investments to areas of personal expertise (e.g. consumer goods, insurance, retail) and avoiding highly technical or unpredictable fields (e.g. biotech, early-stage tech, complex derivatives), investors avoid catastrophic mistakes. This discipline is essential for maintaining a margin of safety and avoiding the cognitive bias of overconfidence.
