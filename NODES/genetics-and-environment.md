# Genetics and Environment

## Core idea
Genes do not determine our destiny; instead, they determine our areas of opportunity. As physician Gabor Mate noted, "Genes can predispose, but they don't predetermine." The strength of genetics is also their weakness: genes cannot be easily changed, meaning they provide a massive advantage in favorable environments and a massive disadvantage in unfavorable ones. Competence is highly context-dependent. The people at the top of any competitive field are not only well trained, but also well suited to the task, aligning their ambition with their natural abilities.

## Why it matters / connection
Understanding the intersection of genetics and environment clarifies where we should direct our hard work. Biological differences matter, but rather than using genetics as an excuse for failure, we should focus on choosing the right environment and playing field to maximize our unique natural talents and reach the ceiling of our capabilities.
