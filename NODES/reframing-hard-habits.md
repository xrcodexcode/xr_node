# Reframing Hard Habits

## Core idea
Reframing Hard Habits is a cognitive method where you shift your language from 'I have to' to 'I get to,' changing how you perceive the friction of a routine.

## Why it matters / connection
Language shapes our emotional state and cravings. Reframing a constraint as an opportunity (e.g., 'I get to exercise' instead of 'I have to exercise') highlights the positive benefits of the habit, reducing psychological friction. For example, instead of thinking 'I have to save money,' reframing it as 'saving money is buying my future freedom' shifts your identity from sacrifice to empowerment, making the habit satisfying.
