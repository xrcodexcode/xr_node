# National Indemnity Acquisition

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway's acquisition of National Indemnity in 1967 for $8.6 million marked Buffett's entry into the insurance sector, securing the foundation of Berkshire's float model.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett purchased National Indemnity from Jack Ringwalt. This acquisition provided Berkshire with a source of low-cost capital (float) that could be reinvested in equities, shifting Berkshire's core business model from declining textiles to capital allocation.
