# Strike the Shepherd and the Sheep Will Scatter

## Core idea
Trouble can often be traced to a single strong individual—the stirrer, the arrogant underling, the poisoner of goodwill. If you allow such people room to act, others will succumb to their influence. Do not wait for the troubles they cause to multiply, do not try to negotiate with them—they are irredeemable. Neutralize their influence by isolating or banishing them. Strike at the source of the trouble and the sheep will scatter.

## Why it matters / connection
In ancient Athens, the system of ostracism was used to banish any citizen who grew too powerful or popular, preventing the rise of tyrants. By striking the 'shepherd'—the single leader around whom opposition could organize—the city-state preserved stability. If you neutralize the one instigating the trouble, the followers lose direction and disband.
