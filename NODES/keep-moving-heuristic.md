# Keep Moving Heuristic

The Keep Moving Heuristic is a career progress rule stating that taking action in *any* direction is superior to remaining paralyzed by choices, because path correction is only possible once an object is in motion.

## Explanation

Young adults often stall their careers trying to construct the "perfect plan." However, complex plans made in isolation lack real-world feedback. When stuck in career confusion, the optimal strategy is to pick a direction and act immediately, treating it as a reversible decision.

If the chosen path turns out to be wrong, you can pivot or step back. This constant motion generates real-world data, exposes hidden opportunities, and builds resilience. Paralysis, on the other hand, yields zero data and breeds anxiety. Feeling stuck is not a lack of options, but rather a choice overload that halts movement.

## Why It Matters

Enforcing action over planning resolves career confusion. By moving constantly, you test assumptions in real time, build professional momentum, and naturally discover the right career fit.
