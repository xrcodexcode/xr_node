# One Percent Better Every Day

## Core idea
Habits represent the compound interest of self-improvement. The effects of habits multiply as they are repeated over days, months, and years. Mathematically:
- **1% better every day for a year**: $1.01^{365} \approx 37.78$ (ending up nearly 38 times better).
- **1% worse every day for a year**: $0.99^{365} \approx 0.03$ (declining nearly down to zero).

Making a choice that is 1% better or 1% worse seems minor in the moment, but over a lifetime, these choices compound to create the massive difference between who we are and who we could be. A slight trajectory change (like an airplane shifting 3.5 degrees south when leaving Los Angeles, causing it to land in Washington, D.C., instead of New York City) leads to completely different destinations.

Outcomes are a lagging measure of habits:
- Net worth is a lagging measure of financial habits.
- Weight is a lagging measure of eating habits.
- Knowledge is a lagging measure of learning habits.
- Clutter is a lagging measure of cleaning habits.

## Why it matters / connection
This concept underscores the importance of trajectory over immediate results. It discourages the expectation of rapid, overnight transformations and encourages patience with slow progress. It warns that daily minor errors compound into toxic results, while daily small wins compound into extraordinary achievements. Time magnifies the margin between success and failure; it multiplies whatever we feed it.
