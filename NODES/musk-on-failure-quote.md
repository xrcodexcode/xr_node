# "Failure is an option here. If things are not failing, you are not innovating enough."

Elon Musk — on the necessity of failure for breakthrough innovation.

## Context

Musk said this in various forms during interviews and all-hands meetings. The most cited version comes from a 2013 interview. He was explaining why SpaceX attempted ambitious designs (like landing rockets) even when initial attempts failed. The quote encapsulates his philosophy that innovation velocity requires accepting a high failure rate.

## Meaning

This is not a defense of sloppiness. It is a structural argument: if zero experiments are failing, the organization is playing too safe. The optimal failure rate for an innovative organization is non-zero. Musk contrasts this with NASA's post-Apollo culture, which became so risk-averse that innovation slowed dramatically.

## Practical application

Leaders should:
- Distinguish between execution failures (bad) and innovation failures (good)
- Set explicit failure targets for R&D teams
- Fire people who hide failures, not people who fail
- Celebrate learning from failure, not just success
