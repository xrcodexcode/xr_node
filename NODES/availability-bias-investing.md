# Availability Bias in Investing

## Core idea
Availability Bias is the cognitive tendency to estimate the probability of an event based on how easily examples of it come to mind. In investing, this causes people to overvalue recent news, market downturns, or dramatic events.

## Why it matters / connection
When the stock market crashes, the media is filled with doom-and-gloom reports. This makes the possibility of a permanent depression feel highly likely (available) to investors, causing them to sell at the bottom. Buffett counters this bias by tuning out daily news and focusing strictly on a company's long-term financial statements.
