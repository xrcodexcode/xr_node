# Warren Buffett's Childhood Entrepreneurship

## Core idea
From an early age, Warren Buffett engaged in multiple small ventures—such as selling chewing gum door-to-door, selling Coca-Cola bottles, retrieving golf balls, and running paper routes—to systematically accumulate and compound cash capital.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett's early experiences formed his foundational understanding of profit margins, customer demand, and capital accumulation. By age 16, his paper route network and other ventures had yielded a net worth of over $5,000 (roughly equivalent to $70,000+ today). This early capital base allowed him to invest in stocks, farmland, and businesses before entering university, demonstrating how early micro-ventures compound into life-changing asset bases.
