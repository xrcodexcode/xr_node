# Elon Musk Education

Musk studied physics and economics across three institutions in Canada and the US, then dropped out of a Stanford PhD after two days to start Zip2.

## Explanation

Musk moved to Canada at 17, attending Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario for two years (1989-1991). He transferred to the University of Pennsylvania on a scholarship, earning two bachelor's degrees: a B.A. in Physics from the College of Arts and Sciences and a B.A. in Economics from the Wharton School (1992-1995).

He entered Stanford University in 1995 for a PhD in physics but left after two days to pursue internet business opportunities during the early web boom.

## Why it matters

The physics + economics combination is unusual and foundational to Musk's thinking. Physics gave him [[first-principles-thinking]] and a mental framework for problem-solving. Economics gave him business intuition. He has said: "Physics gives you a mental framework for problem-solving. It also teaches you to be okay with being wrong."
