# Discover Each Man's Thumbscrew

## Core idea
Everyone has a weakness, a gap in the castle wall. That weakness is usually an insecurity, an uncontrollable emotion or need; it can also be a small secret pleasure. Either way, once found, it is a thumbscrew you can turn to your advantage.

## Why it matters / connection
Cardinal Richelieu discovered that King Louis XIII's weakness was his profound insecurity and need to feel in control. Richelieu would subtly present options that made the King feel decisive and powerful, while actually directing all state policy himself. In business, art dealer Joseph Duveen targeted the desire of American tycoons for aristocratic pedigree, selling them European masters to satisfy their social ambitions.
