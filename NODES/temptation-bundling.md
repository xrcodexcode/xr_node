# Temptation Bundling

## Core idea
Temptation bundling is a strategy to make habits more attractive by linking an action you need to do (a low-probability behavior) with an action you want to do (a high-probability behavior). 

- **Ronan Byrne's Hacked Bike**: An electrical engineering student in Dublin wanted to watch Netflix but knew he needed to exercise. He hacked his stationary bike and wrote a computer program that only allowed Netflix to run if he was pedaling above a certain speed. If he slowed down, the show paused.
- **ABC's TGIT Campaign**: The American Broadcasting Company promoted "Thank God It's Thursday" (TGIT) by branding a three-hour block of Shonda Rhimes shows (*Grey's Anatomy*, *Scandal*, *How to Get Away with Murder*) and encouraging viewers to make popcorn, drink red wine, and enjoy the evening. This bundled the action ABC needed (viewers watching their shows) with things viewers already wanted to do (relax, drink wine, eat popcorn).
- **Premack's Principle**: This strategy is a practical application of Premack's Principle, which states that "more probable behaviors will reinforce less probable behaviors."
- **Habit Stacking + Temptation Bundling Formula**:
  By combining these two strategies, you can create a clear set of rules to guide your behavior:
  1. *After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [HABIT I NEED].*
  2. *After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT].*
  
  *Examples*:
  - If you want to check Facebook but need to exercise: "After I pull out my phone, I will do ten burpees (need). After I do ten burpees, I will check Facebook (want)."
  - If you want to watch sports but need to make sales calls: "After I get back from my lunch break, I will call three potential clients (need). After I call three potential clients, I will check ESPN (want)."

## Why it matters / connection
It is difficult to build self-discipline through sheer willpower. By engineering a habit stack that pairs a necessary habit with an immediate reward (something you want to do), you create a dopamine spike in anticipation of the reward. Doing the thing you need to do becomes the gateway to doing the thing you want to do, making the habit irresistible.
