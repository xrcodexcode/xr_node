# Time Blocking Productivity Method

Musk divides his day into 5-minute time blocks, assigning every minute to a specific task or person. He works in intense focus bursts.

## Explanation

Musk manages his schedule in 5-minute increments, planning his week in advance down to this granularity. He works ~85-100 hours per week, splitting time across Tesla, SpaceX, xAI, X, Neuralink, and The Boring Company.

His typical weekly time allocation (approximate, varies):
- Tesla: ~40 hours
- SpaceX: ~35 hours
- xAI/X: ~15 hours
- Neuralink/Boring Co: ~5-10 hours

He is known for intense focus: when working on a specific problem, he goes "deeply into the rabbit hole" for hours, ignoring everything else. He also uses "batching" — grouping similar tasks together to minimize context-switching costs.

## Why it matters

Time blocking at 5-minute granularity is unusual. Most executives allocate in 30-60 minute blocks. Musk's approach reflects his belief that time is the scarcest resource and must be allocated with [[first-principles-thinking]] rigor.
