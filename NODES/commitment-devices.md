# Commitment Devices

## Core idea
A commitment device (sometimes known as a Ulysses pact) is a choice made in the present that controls or locks in future behaviors. It is the inversion of the 3rd Law of Behavior Change: **Make It Difficult**. By creating a commitment device, you increase the friction of bad behaviors in the present, making them difficult or impossible to perform, while locking in good habits.

Examples of commitment devices include:
- **Health**: Purchasing food in individual packages rather than bulk sizes to prevent overeating, or leaving your wallet at home to avoid buying fast food.
- **Sleep**: Using an outlet timer that automatically cuts off the power to the internet router at 10 p.m. to prompt bedtime.
- **Finance**: Setting up automatic savings transfers or automatic bill pay so you never have to think about saving or paying bills on time.
- **Focus**: Asking an assistant or friend to reset passwords on social media accounts on Monday morning, locking you out until the weekend.

Commitment devices are highly effective because they allow individuals to act on their good intentions while in a rational, cool-headed state before they can fall victim to future emotional temptations or cravings.

## Why it matters / connection
Willpower alone is often insufficient when temptation is close at hand. Commitment devices bypass the need for continuous willpower by engineering the environment to automate good behaviors and restrict bad ones. They represent a key method for making good habits inevitable and bad habits impossible.
