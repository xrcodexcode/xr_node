# Reinforcement Principle

## Core idea
Reinforcement is the process of encouraging or establishing a behavior by presenting a reward immediately after the behavior is executed, strengthening the habit loop.

## Why it matters / connection
The brain uses reinforcement to learn which actions are worth repeating. To make a habit stick, the reward must be immediate. Even a small, symbolic reward (like crossing an item off a checklist or a minor treat) satisfies the craving and reinforces the routine.
