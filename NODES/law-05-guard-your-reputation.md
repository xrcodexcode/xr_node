# So Much Depends on Reputation — Guard It with Your Life

## Core idea
Reputation is the cornerstone of power. Through reputation alone you can win and intimidate; once it slips, however, you are vulnerable and will be attacked on all sides. Make your reputation unassailable. Always be alert to potential attacks and thwart them before they happen.

## Why it matters / connection
During the Three Kingdoms period, Chinese strategist Chuko Liang repelled a force of 150,000 soldiers by simply sitting at the gates of an empty city, playing his lute. The enemy general, knowing Liang's reputation for extreme caution and tactical genius, assumed it was a trap and retreated. P.T. Barnum also protected his reputation fiercely, planting stories to destroy competitors before they could attack him.
