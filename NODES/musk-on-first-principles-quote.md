# "I think it's important to reason from first principles rather than by analogy. The normal way we conduct our lives is we reason by analogy. You boil things down to the most fundamental truths you can imagine, and then reason up from there."

Elon Musk — the canonical articulation of his core thinking method.

## Context

Said during an interview with Kevin Rose in 2013. This interview popularized "first principles thinking" in business and startup culture. Musk was explaining why he believed SpaceX could build rockets cheaper than established aerospace companies.

## Meaning

Most people reason by analogy: "this is how others do it, therefore this is how it must be done." First principles reasoning deconstructs the problem to physics-level fundamentals and rebuilds from there. Analogy produces incremental improvement; first principles produces breakthroughs.

## Practical application

When facing a problem:
1. Identify received assumptions
2. Strip away everything that's convention, not physics
3. Find the fundamental truths
4. Rebuild the solution from those truths
5. Ignore "that's how it's always been done"
