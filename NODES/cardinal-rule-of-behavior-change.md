# The Cardinal Rule of Behavior Change

## Core idea
The Cardinal Rule of Behavior Change states: **What is immediately rewarded is repeated. What is immediately punished is avoided.** 

This rule highlights the difference in how the habit loop is closed across time:
- The first three laws of behavior change (make it obvious, make it attractive, make it easy) increase the odds that a behavior will be performed *this time*.
- The fourth law of behavior change (make it satisfying) increases the odds that a behavior will be repeated *next time*.

Without immediate reinforcement, there is no pleasure signal to teach the brain that a behavior is worth remembering and repeating.

## Why it matters / connection
This rule is the foundation of habit sustainability. Understanding it explains why bad habits (which offer immediate rewards but delayed costs) are easy to repeat, while good habits (which offer delayed rewards but immediate costs) are easy to avoid. To build lasting habits, we must bring the rewards of good behaviors and the punishments of bad behaviors into the present moment.
