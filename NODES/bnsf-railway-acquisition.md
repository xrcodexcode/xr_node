# BNSF Railway Acquisition

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway acquired Burlington Northern Santa Fe (BNSF) Railway in 2009 for $26 billion, representing a massive bet on US industrial infrastructure and capital-intensive compounding.

## Why it matters / connection
While railroads are capital-intensive, they possess an irreplaceable geographical moat (you cannot build a competing track). BNSF serves as an efficient capital sink, allowing Berkshire to reinvest billions of dollars internally at steady, regulated rates of return.
