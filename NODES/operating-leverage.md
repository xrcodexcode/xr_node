# Concept of Operating Leverage

## Core idea
Operating Leverage is a measure of how revenue growth translates into growth in operating income, driven by a business model's proportion of fixed costs versus variable costs.

## Why it matters / connection
Businesses with high fixed costs and low variable costs (like software, rail, or utility companies) experience rapid profit growth once revenues cross the break-even threshold, because additional sales incur near-zero incremental costs. Buffett seeks businesses with high operating leverage that require minimal capital expenditure to scale.
