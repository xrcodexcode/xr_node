# James Clear on Clarity

## Core idea
James Clear asserts: 'Many people think they lack motivation when what they really lack is clarity.' He advocates for highly specific implementation intentions.

## Why it matters / connection
Without a clear cue of when and where to act, the brain remains in decision paralysis, wasting cognitive energy on deciding *if* it should perform the habit. Specifying the exact time and location of a habit (e.g., 'I will meditate for 5 minutes at 7:00 AM in my living room') eliminates the need for willpower. It turns the action into an automatic response to a situational trigger, bypassing motivation entirely.
