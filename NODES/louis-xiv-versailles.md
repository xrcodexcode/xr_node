# Louis XIV and Versailles

## Core idea
King Louis XIV of France used the palace of Versailles as a strategic instrument of political control. By forcing the French nobility to live under his roof, he transformed potential rebels into domestic courtiers obsessed with court etiquette, titles, and royal favor.

## Why it matters / connection
Versailles was a massive golden cage. Before its construction, the French nobles lived on their provincial estates, commanding private armies and posing a constant threat of rebellion. At Versailles, Louis XIV subjected them to an elaborate, daily system of rituals (such as the *lever* and *coucher*) where proximity to the King was the ultimate status. He made them spend lavishly on clothes and gambling, leaving them financially dependent on royal pensions and incapable of organizing opposition.
