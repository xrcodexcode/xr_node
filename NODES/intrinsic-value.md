# Concept of Intrinsic Value

## Core idea
Intrinsic Value is the actual estimated worth of a business, calculated as the present value of all future cash flows expected to be generated by the business over its remaining life, discounted back to the present.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett defines intrinsic value as the only logical metric for evaluating the attractiveness of an acquisition or investment. It is calculated using a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model:
\[V = \sum_{t=1}^{\infty} \frac{CF_t}{(1 + r)^t}\]
where \(CF_t\) represents the free cash flow in year \(t\) and \(r\) represents the discount rate (traditionally the long-term US Treasury bond yield to represent risk-free rate, though Buffett adjusts it if interest rates are abnormally low).
While market price is highly volatile, intrinsic value changes slowly based on business fundamentals. Understanding this distinction allows investors to identify when market pricing is rational and when it is offering a bargain.
