# Do Not Build Fortresses to Protect Yourself — Isolation is Dangerous

## Core idea
The world is dangerous and enemies are everywhere—everyone has to protect themselves. A fortress seems the safest. But isolation exposes you to more dangers than it protects you from—it cuts you off from valuable information, it makes you conspicuous and an easy target. Better to circulate among people, find allies, mingle.

## Why it matters / connection
Ch'in Shih Huang Ti, the First Emperor of China, was so terrified of assassins that he slept in a different room of his massive palace every night, hidden from everyone. He became isolated, his ministers lied to him, and he eventually died alone and out of touch with his empire. In contrast, Louis XIV used Versailles not to isolate himself, but to force the entire nobility to live with him, keeping them under his nose.
