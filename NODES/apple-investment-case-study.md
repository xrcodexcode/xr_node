# Apple Investment Case Study

## Core idea
Berkshire Hathaway's massive investment in Apple, beginning in 2016 and growing to become its largest equity holding, represents Warren Buffett's application of consumer brand moats to the technology sector, treating Apple as a consumer sticky ecosystem rather than a tech hardware company.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett historically avoided tech stocks (claiming they were outside his circle of competence). However, he observed that the iPhone was not just a device, but an essential utility with extreme customer loyalty and massive switching costs (ecosystem lock-in). This strong moat gave Apple pricing power, while its high capital return model (significant stock buybacks) aligned perfectly with Buffett's capital allocation principles. By purchasing Apple, Buffett demonstrated that value investing can adapt to modern digital platforms if they possess durable competitive moats.
