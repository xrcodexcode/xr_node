# Genes Predispose but Do Not Determine

## Core idea
This principle states that genes predispose us to certain strengths, vulnerabilities, and temperament profiles, but do not determine our absolute outcomes in life.

## Why it matters / connection
Genes define our areas of natural advantage and ease. By choosing habits and careers that align with our genetic predispositions (our biological strengths), we make the response easy. For example, Michael Phelps has an ideal body for swimming (long torso, short legs), while Hicham El Guerrouj has an ideal body for running (long legs, short torso). If they swapped sports, they would struggle. By playing a game that favors our biology, we compound success much faster, turning hard routines into natural habits.
