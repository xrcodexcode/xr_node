# Evidence of Exceptional Ability Hiring

Musk hires based on demonstrated evidence of exceptional ability, not credentials, years of experience, or pedigree. He personally interviews every early engineer.

## Explanation

Musk's hiring heuristic: look for people who have done remarkable things, regardless of their background. He values:

- Direct evidence of hard-core engineering ability (personal projects, open-source contributions, competition wins)
- Problem-solving approach over domain knowledge ("I'd rather hire someone who's never built a rocket but can solve hard physics problems")
- Obsessive dedication (long hours, deep focus)
- Mission alignment (they must believe in the company's purpose)

He personally interviews candidates for key roles, sometimes late at night. His interview questions focus on real engineering problems rather than behavioral questions. At SpaceX, he is known for asking "You're standing on a factory floor with a fire hose. How do you put out the fire?" — testing practical problem-solving under pressure.

## Why it matters

Traditional hiring filters for credentials and experience, which biases toward conventional candidates. Musk's approach lets him find exceptional people who would be filtered out by conventional HR processes. This is a direct application of [[talent-density-principle]].
