# Question Every Requirement

All requirements should be treated as suggestions, not laws. Even requirements from smart people may be wrong, outdated, or based on flawed assumptions.

## Explanation

This is step 1 of Musk's five-step engineering algorithm. He insists that every requirement must be traceable back to who originated it and why. If the original author can't be identified or the reasoning is weak, the requirement should be deleted.

Musk's rule: "If a requirement is from a smart person, question it anyway. Smart people make the most dangerous assumptions because they're convincing."

At SpaceX, Musk found the supply chain was buying components to specifications written 30 years ago by people who were no longer at the company. No one could explain why the specs existed. They were deleted, and costs dropped.

## Why it matters

Organizations accumulate requirements over time like sedimentary layers. Most become obsolete but persist through inertia. Questioning everything reveals which are still necessary and which are costing money and time without justification.
