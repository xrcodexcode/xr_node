# Margin of Safety Principle

## Core idea
The Margin of Safety is the difference between a business's intrinsic value and its current stock market price. Buying an asset at a substantial discount to its intrinsic value provides a cushion that protects the investor's capital against forecasting errors, market shocks, or business deterioration.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett describes the margin of safety as the three most important words in investing. If an investor calculates the intrinsic value of a business to be $100 per share, they do not buy it at $95. They wait until the market price falls to $60 or $70. This gap absorbs the inevitable inaccuracy of estimating future cash flows. It ensures that even if the business performs slightly worse than expected, the investment is still likely to yield a positive return, preventing permanent capital loss.
