# Authority Bias in Investing

## Core idea
Authority Bias is the psychological tendency to overvalue the opinions of perceived experts or authority figures, regardless of their actual performance record or underlying logic.

## Why it matters / connection
Investors often herd into stocks based on Wall Street analyst upgrades, central bank statements, or academic theories. Buffett and Munger counter this by performing independent, first-principles analysis, relying on primary documents (like annual reports) rather than analyst reports.
