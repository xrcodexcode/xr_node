# Dempster Mill Investment

## Core idea
Warren Buffett purchased a controlling stake in Dempster Mill Manufacturing Company, a manufacturer of windmills and water pumps, in 1961 for his investment partnership. The company was struggling operationally.

## Why it matters / connection
Buffett originally bought Dempster as a cheap 'cigar butt' stock trading below net working capital. When management resisted cost cuts, Buffett hired turnaround specialist Harry Bottle, who slashed inventory, reduced overhead, and freed up capital to be reinvested in higher-yielding securities, demonstrating active capital restructuring.
