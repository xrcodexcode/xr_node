# Algorithm Design Sequence

Musk's five-step engineering algorithm: Question → Delete → Simplify → Accelerate → Automate. Must be followed in strict order.

## Explanation

| Step | Action | Common Mistake |
|------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Question every requirement | Treating requirements as sacred |
| 2 | Delete any part/process you can | Hesitation to remove ("we might need it") |
| 3 | Simplify and optimize | Optimizing before deleting |
| 4 | Accelerate | Accelerating before simplifying |
| 5 | Automate | Automating a process that shouldn't exist |

The test for step 2: if you delete something and it's not added back at least 10% of the time, you didn't delete enough. This forces aggressive removal.

## Why it matters

This is Musk's most explicit engineering framework. It prevents the most common manufacturing failure mode: automating or accelerating waste. It also reflects his belief that most corporate processes grow by addition, never subtraction.
