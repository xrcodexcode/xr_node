# Fermi Estimation

Use order-of-magnitude calculations to determine if a goal is physically possible before investing engineering resources. Named for physicist Enrico Fermi.

## Explanation

Musk routinely uses back-of-envelope Fermi calculations to filter ideas. Before detailed engineering begins, he estimates rough orders of magnitude: What tonnage to Mars? How many Starship launches? What launch cost per kg? What would that imply about cost targets?

The purpose is not precision but triage: if the Fermi estimate shows an idea is off by 100x, it's discarded. If it's within range of possible, engineering proceeds.

## Why it matters

Prevents "analysis paralysis" on impossible projects. Separates the merely ambitious from the genuinely impossible. Builds intuition for what is physically reachable.
