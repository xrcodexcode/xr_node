# AI Existential Risk Philosophy

Musk believes unconstrained AGI development is the single greatest existential threat to humanity, potentially more dangerous than nuclear weapons.

## Explanation

Musk has consistently warned about AI risk since at least 2014, calling AI "summoning the demon." His reasoning: if a superintelligent AI has goals misaligned with human values, and is vastly more capable than humans, we cannot survive a conflict. He estimates a 10-20% probability of AI causing human extinction.

His actions to mitigate this risk:
- Co-founded OpenAI (2015) as a non-profit to develop AI safely
- Left OpenAI (2018) when it shifted to for-profit
- Founded xAI (2023) to build a "maximally curious" AI seeking truth
- Invested in Neuralink (2016) to create human-AI symbiosis

## Why it matters

This belief drives Musk's AI-related business decisions and explains his competitive stance against OpenAI/Google. It's a key example of [[bayesian-decision-making]] applied to civilization-level risk — even a 10% extinction risk merits massive countermeasure investment.
