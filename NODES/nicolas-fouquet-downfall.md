# The Downfall of Nicolas Fouquet

## Core idea
The arrest and life imprisonment of Nicolas Fouquet, Louis XIV's finance minister, is the classic historical warning against outshining the master. His desire to impress the King with his wealth and taste triggered Louis's insecurity, leading to his ruin.

## Why it matters / connection
Fouquet built the magnificent estate of Vaux-le-Vicomte and threw a grand party in August 1661 to honor the young King. The entertainment, gardens, and food were so superior to anything the King owned that Louis felt public humiliation and envy. Three weeks later, Fouquet was arrested by d'Artagnan on charges of embezzlement. The King confiscated Fouquet's architects and artists to build Versailles, and Fouquet spent the remaining twenty years of his life in isolated confinement.
