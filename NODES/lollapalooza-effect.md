# Lollapalooza Effect

## Core idea
The Lollapalooza Effect, coined by Charlie Munger, describes the confluence of multiple psychological biases acting in the same direction, which triggers an extremely powerful scale of human action or outcome.

## Why it matters / connection
In business and investing, understanding this effect helps explain massive market bubbles, panics, or product successes. For example, open-cry auctions combine social proof, commitment bias, scarcity, and authority, leading to irrational bidding wars. Recognizing how biases compound prevents investors from falling victim to herd behavior.
