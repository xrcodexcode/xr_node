# Implementation Intentions

## Core idea
An implementation intention is a plan made beforehand about when and where to act. It is formulated to pair a new behavior with two of the most common environmental cues: time and location. The general formula is:
* "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."
* "When situation X arises, I will perform response Y."

In a 2001 study in Great Britain tracking 248 people building exercise habits, researchers found that motivation alone was ineffective. The control group and a group presented with motivational material about cardiovascular health had only a 35 to 38 percent success rate. However, the third group—which formulated an implementation intention stating exactly when and where they would exercise—achieved a 91 percent success rate.

Similar studies show that implementation intentions dramatically increase voter turnout (by asking voters their route, time, and transit plan) and improve compliance with tax payments, medical appointments, and recycling.

## Why it matters / connection
Many people believe they lack motivation to change, but what they actually lack is clarity. Vague goals like "I want to eat healthier" leave action up to chance. An implementation intention eliminates decision-making in the moment of action; instead of waiting for inspiration or willpower, you simply execute a predetermined plan.
