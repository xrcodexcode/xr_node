# Prototype-Then-Iterate

Build a working prototype as fast as possible, test it in real conditions, learn from failure, iterate rapidly. Speed of learning > perfection on first attempt.

## Explanation

Musk rejects the traditional aerospace approach of exhaustive planning, simulation, and testing before building. Instead, his companies build fast, test aggressively, and iterate based on what breaks.

SpaceX built and flew three Falcon 1 rockets before reaching orbit. Each failure taught something: the first failed from a fuel-line issue, the second from stage separation. On the fourth attempt, Falcon 1 reached orbit in 2008, saving the company from bankruptcy.

Tesla applies the same: over-the-air software updates enable continuous improvement after the product is in customers' hands. "We fix problems as fast as we find them."

## Why it matters

Real-world testing reveals problems that simulation never catches. The cost of fast prototyping is failures. The benefit is compressed learning cycles: SpaceX went from nothing to orbit in ~6 years, a timeline traditionally requiring government-scale budgets.
