# Idiot Index

If a product costs more than its raw materials, the gap measures how much "idiot" — unnecessary complexity, supply chain markup, or industry convention — is built into the price.

## Explanation

The Idiot Index is Musk's heuristic for identifying manufacturing inefficiencies. Calculate it as: total cost of the finished part divided by the cost of the raw materials. If the ratio is high (e.g., 20x for aerospace parts), there is room for dramatic cost reduction through in-house manufacturing, vertical integration, or process redesign.

Musk applied this to rockets: a traditional rocket cost $60M+, but raw materials were ~$2M (index of 30x). SpaceX's response: build in-house, control manufacturing, design for reusability.

## Why it matters

The Idiot Index provides a quick triage: which parts should the company make vs. buy? A high index signals that the market price includes significant inefficiency that can be disrupted. A low index (~2-3x) suggests the market is already efficient.
