# Long-Term Time Horizon

Think in decades and centuries, not quarters. Short-term pain is acceptable if it enables long-term mission achievement.

## Explanation

Musk operates on an unusual time horizon: Tesla's mission (accelerate sustainable energy) requires 30+ years. SpaceX's Mars colonization mission requires 50+ years. He evaluates decisions against these timeframes, not quarterly earnings.

This has concrete consequences:
- Tesla invested in Gigafactories before profitability
- SpaceX developed reusable rockets over 15+ years of R&D
- Both companies accepted years of negative cash flow
- Tesla opened its patents in 2014 — a short-term disadvantage for long-term ecosystem growth

Musk told a venture capitalist in 2004 exactly what Tesla would become. The VC thought he was insane. Musk saw 20 years ahead.

## Why it matters

A longer time horizon enables strategies that look irrational on quarterly metrics but dominate over decades: vertical integration, massive capital investment, open patenting, and radical R&D bets.
